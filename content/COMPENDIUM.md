# doswiki — The Compendium

**The full universe of health, fitness, and longevity compounds — approved and non-approved.**
Organised by what you want to achieve. Every entry names the molecular target (with an official gene/compound database link), explains the mechanism technically, and then in plain English.

*Last researched: July 2026. This is educational content, not medical advice. Nothing here is a recommendation to use any substance. Non-approved and controlled substances are documented for completeness and harm-reduction, not endorsement.*

---

## How to read every entry

- **Approval badge** — legal/regulatory status (see key below)
- **Evidence** — strength of *human* evidence (see key below)
- **Technical mechanism** — the receptor / enzyme / gene pathway, named precisely
- **Molecular target** — an official database link for the gene or protein it acts on, plus a PubChem link for the compound itself
- **In plain English** — the same mechanism for a non-scientist
- **Protocol / Watch out / Bottom line** — practical use, risks, honest verdict

### Approval key
- 🟢 **FDA Approved** — approved for the stated use
- 🟡 **OTC Supplement** — legal dietary supplement (regulated, not "approved" for disease claims)
- 🔵 **Prescription Only** — legal with a doctor's prescription
- 🟠 **Off-Label** — an approved drug used for an unapproved purpose
- 🔴 **Not Approved** — no approval for human use (research chemicals, most peptides, SARMs)
- ⚫ **Controlled Substance** — scheduled under narcotics/controlled-drugs law

### Evidence key
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ multiple large human RCTs, consistent
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ good RCT evidence, minor inconsistency
- ⭐⭐⭐ moderate — some RCTs, mixed or small
- ⭐⭐ mostly animal data or single small human trials
- ⭐ theoretical / anecdotal / no controlled human data

### A note on the 2026 peptide landscape
In **April 2026** the FDA removed 12 peptides — including **BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu (injectable), MOTS-C, Epitalon, LL-37, KPV, DSIP, Semax, Melanotan II, DiHexa, PEG-MGF** — from its Category 2 "significant safety risk" list. Removal is **not** approval and **not** OTC legality — it only lifts the explicit prohibition. A separate FDA PCAC review on **July 23–24, 2026** is deciding whether BPC-157, TB-500, MOTS-C, KPV, DSIP, Semax and Epitalon become eligible for 503A pharmacy compounding. Until that concludes, these remain **not approved** and legally grey. Status noted per-entry below.
*Sources: [PeptideWise 503A list](https://www.getpeptidewise.com/blog/fda-peptide-reclassification-2026/), [Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/the-peptide-reclassification-everyone-s-talking-about-a-pharmacist-s-take-on-what-rfk-jr-s-announcement-actually-means).*

# CATEGORY 1 — FOUNDATIONAL / GENERAL HEALTH

These are the highest-evidence, lowest-risk compounds. Most people are deficient in several.

---

### Creatine Monohydrate  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Build muscle · Strength · Power · Brain energy (aging, vegetarians)
**Technical mechanism:** Creatine is taken into muscle by the **SLC6A8** creatine transporter and phosphorylated to phosphocreatine by creatine kinase (**CKM**). Phosphocreatine donates a phosphate to ADP to regenerate ATP during efforts of 1–10 seconds — the phosphagen energy system. Supplementation raises intramuscular phosphocreatine ~20%, extending peak power before fatigue. In brain, it buffers ATP in high-demand neurons.
**Molecular target:** [SLC6A8 creatine transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6535) · [Creatine (PubChem CID 586)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/586) · [Examine: creatine](https://examine.com/supplements/creatine/)
**In plain English:** Your muscles have a tiny rechargeable battery for explosive effort. Creatine makes that battery bigger, so you get 1–2 extra hard reps and recover faster between sets. It's the most proven, cheapest sports supplement that exists.
**Protocol:** 3–5 g/day, monohydrate, any time. No loading needed.
**Watch out:** 1–2 kg water weight in week one (not fat). Safe long-term in healthy kidneys.
**Bottom line:** If you take one supplement, this is it.
**Stacks with:** Whey/protein and carbs post-workout (the insulin bump helps uptake).
**Cost:** The cheapest effective supplement — ~S$20–40 for 3–6 months of monohydrate. Buy plain “creatine monohydrate” (Creapure is a quality mark); skip pricey “advanced” forms.

### Caffeine  🟢/🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Focus · Endurance · Strength · Fat loss · Alertness
**Technical mechanism:** Competitive antagonist at **adenosine A1 and A2A receptors (ADORA2A)**. Adenosine accumulates during wakefulness and signals fatigue; blocking it lifts the brakes on dopaminergic and cholinergic signalling, raises catecholamines, and lowers perceived exertion. Also mobilises free fatty acids, sparing glycogen.
**Molecular target:** [ADORA2A adenosine A2A receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/135) · [Caffeine (PubChem CID 2519)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2519)
**In plain English:** Adenosine is the "you're tired" molecule that builds up all day. Caffeine plugs into its parking spots so the tired signal can't land — you feel alert and exercise feels easier.
**Protocol:** 3–6 mg/kg, 45–60 min pre-effort. Cycle off 10–14 days every ~2 months.
**Watch out:** 5–6 h half-life (up to 9 h in slow metabolisers) — afternoon doses wreck deep sleep.
**Bottom line:** With creatine, one of the two most proven performance aids on earth.
**Stacks with:** L-theanine (~200 mg) to smooth the jitters without dulling the focus.
**Avoid:** Other strong stimulants (yohimbine, high-dose synephrine) — additive heart-rate and blood-pressure load. Keep it >8–10 h from bedtime. Go easy if you’re prone to anxiety.
**Cost:** Pennies — caffeine tablets are ~S$10–20 for months, or just coffee.

---

### L-Theanine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (with caffeine)
**Goals:** Focus · Calm · Sleep
**Technical mechanism:** Amino acid from tea that crosses the blood-brain barrier and increases **GABA**, glycine, and alpha-wave EEG activity while modulating glutamate (NMDA/AMPA) tone. Blunts the sympathetic overshoot of caffeine without reducing its focus benefit.
**Molecular target:** [GABRA1 GABA-A receptor subunit (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2554) · [L-Theanine (PubChem CID 439378)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/439378)
**In plain English:** It's the "calm" molecule in green tea. Paired with caffeine, it removes the jitter and gives you smooth focus instead of a wired buzz.
**Protocol:** 200 mg theanine : 100 mg caffeine for focus; 200–400 mg alone for sleep.
**Bottom line:** The most practical no-prescription focus stack.
**Stacks with:** Caffeine — the classic calm-focus pairing (roughly 1:2 caffeine:theanine); removes the jitters without dulling the alertness.
**Cost:** Cheap — ~S$15–25/month. iHerb, Guardian, Shopee.

---

### Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)  🟡/🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (CV) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (recovery)
**Goals:** Heart · Recovery · Mood · Joints · Brain · Longevity
**Technical mechanism:** EPA and DHA incorporate into cell-membrane phospholipids and are converted to anti-inflammatory eicosanoids (resolvins, protectins). They activate **PPARα** and the fatty-acid receptor **FFAR4 (GPR120)**, lower hepatic triglyceride synthesis, and reduce NF-κB-driven cytokines (IL-6, CRP).
**Molecular target:** [FFAR4/GPR120 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/338557) · [PPARA (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5465) · [EPA (PubChem CID 446284)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/446284)
**In plain English:** These are the fish-oil fats your cell walls are built from. They calm inflammation and lower blood fats — most useful if you don't eat oily fish 2–3× a week.
**Protocol:** 1–3 g combined EPA+DHA/day with a fatty meal. Check label — a 1 g capsule may be only 300 mg EPA+DHA.
**Watch out:** Blood-thinning >3 g/day; slight AFib signal at 4 g.
**Bottom line:** Rare supplement with strong evidence across heart, joints, and brain.
**Stacks with:** Take with a fatty meal for absorption; often paired with vitamin D.
**Avoid:** High doses (>3 g/day) alongside blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin) — additive bleeding risk. Stop ~1 week before surgery.
**Cost:** ~S$20–45/month. Buy on total EPA+DHA (read the back), not the “1000 mg fish oil” on the front.

---

### Vitamin D3 (+ K2)  🟡/🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (deficiency)
**Goals:** Hormones · Immunity · Bone · Muscle · Longevity
**Technical mechanism:** Cholecalciferol → 25(OH)D (liver) → 1,25(OH)₂D (kidney), which binds the **VDR (vitamin D receptor)**, a nuclear transcription factor regulating 200+ genes including those for calcium transport, immune peptides, and testosterone synthesis. K2 (MK-7) activates matrix-Gla protein to route calcium into bone, not arteries.
**Molecular target:** [VDR vitamin D receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/7421) · [Cholecalciferol (PubChem CID 5280795)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5280795)
**In plain English:** A hormone your skin makes from sunlight that switches on hundreds of genes. Indoor life (very common in Singapore) leaves most people deficient, which quietly lowers testosterone, immunity, and mood.
**Protocol:** 2,000–4,000 IU D3 + 100–200 mcg K2 with fat; test 25-OH-D, target 75–125 nmol/L.
**Bottom line:** Highest-value fix if you work indoors. Almost everyone benefits.
**Stacks with:** Magnesium (a cofactor that activates vitamin D) and K2 (directs the calcium to bone, not arteries).
**Avoid:** Mega-doses without K2 and magnesium; get a blood level rather than guessing.
**Cost:** Cheap — ~S$10–20/month. Everywhere in SG.

---

### Magnesium  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (sleep/deficiency)
**Goals:** Sleep · Calm · Heart · Recovery · Hormones
**Technical mechanism:** Cofactor for 300+ enzymes including ATP synthesis (ATP is biologically active as Mg-ATP), and a natural NMDA-receptor gate blocker (reduces neural excitation). Modulates the HPA axis, lowering cortisol. 50–60% of adults under-consume it; athletes lose it in sweat.
**Molecular target:** [TRPM6 magnesium transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/140803) · [Magnesium (PubChem CID 5462224)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5462224)
**In plain English:** A mineral your body uses in hundreds of reactions and burns through when you train and stress. Topping it up — especially glycinate at night — reliably improves sleep.
**Protocol:** 200–400 mg elemental. Glycinate (sleep/calm), L-threonate (brain), citrate (constipation). Avoid oxide (4% absorbed).
**Bottom line:** Cheap, safe, most athletes are low. Glycinate before bed.
**Stacks with:** Vitamin D3/K2 (magnesium is a cofactor for activating vitamin D) and vitamin B6 (aids uptake).
**Avoid:** High doses alongside tetracycline or quinolone antibiotics — separate by ~2 hours.
**Cost:** ~S$15–30/month; glycinate/threonate cost more than oxide but absorb better and are gentler on the gut.

### Zinc  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (deficiency/immune)
**Goals:** Testosterone · Immunity · Recovery
**Technical mechanism:** Cofactor in 100+ enzymes and required by Leydig cells for testosterone synthesis; a structural ion in "zinc-finger" transcription factors. Lost in sweat, so athletes are frequently deficient — and deficiency directly suppresses testosterone.
**Molecular target:** [SLC39A / ZIP zinc transporters (NCBI Gene, SLC39A4)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/55630) · [Zinc (PubChem CID 23994)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/23994)
**In plain English:** A mineral your testosterone factory can't run without. Sweat it out training, and hormones drop. Fixing a deficiency reliably raises testosterone; taking extra when you're already fine does nothing.
**Protocol:** 15–30 mg with food (not empty stomach). Add 1–2 mg copper if used for months.
**Bottom line:** Valuable *if deficient*, which many athletes are.
**Stacks with:** Copper (~1 mg per 15 mg zinc) to prevent depletion.
**Avoid:** Long-term high-dose zinc depletes copper; don’t take with calcium, iron or coffee (they compete for absorption).
**Cost:** Cheap — ~S$10–20/month.

### Boron  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Free testosterone · Bone · Joints
**Technical mechanism:** Trace mineral that lowers sex-hormone-binding-globulin (**SHBG**) — freeing bound testosterone — reduces estradiol via aromatase influence, and improves conversion of vitamin D to its active form.
**Molecular target:** [SHBG (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6462) · [Boric acid (PubChem CID 7628)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/7628)
**In plain English:** Most of your testosterone is "handcuffed" to a carrier protein and unusable. Boron loosens those handcuffs so more testosterone is actually active. Cheap and underused.
**Protocol:** 3–10 mg/day with food. >20 mg is toxic.
**Bottom line:** Add 10 mg to a zinc + D3 stack — strong evidence per dollar.

---

### Taurine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (performance, emerging longevity)
**Goals:** Endurance · Heart · Longevity · Calm
**Technical mechanism:** Conditionally essential amino acid concentrating in heart and muscle; stabilises membranes, regulates intracellular calcium handling, is an antioxidant, and acts as a weak GABA-A agonist. A 2023 *Science* study found taurine declines with age and supplementation extended lifespan in mice and monkeys.
**Molecular target:** [SLC6A6 taurine transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6533) · [Taurine (PubChem CID 1123)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/1123)
**In plain English:** An amino acid that keeps heart and muscle cells stable and calm. Its levels fall as you age; topping it up extended healthy lifespan in animals and is cheap and safe in humans.
**Protocol:** 1–3 g/day (up to 6 g in performance studies).
**Bottom line:** Low-risk, promising longevity and endurance support.

---

### N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)  🟡/🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (as antidote) ⭐⭐⭐ (other)
**Goals:** Antioxidant · Liver · Longevity (as GlyNAC) · Mood
**Technical mechanism:** Precursor to cysteine, the rate-limiting amino acid for **glutathione (GSH)** — the body's master antioxidant. Replenishes GSH, modulates glutamate in the brain (obsessive/compulsive circuits), and thins mucus. FDA-approved as the antidote for paracetamol overdose.
**Molecular target:** [GCLC glutamate-cysteine ligase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2729) · [Acetylcysteine (PubChem CID 12035)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/12035)
**In plain English:** Your cells' main antioxidant (glutathione) needs a specific building block that runs low. NAC delivers it, restocking your internal rust-protection — especially for the liver.
**Protocol:** 600–1,200 mg/day. Longevity: pair with glycine ("GlyNAC").
**Bottom line:** Cheap, versatile, genuinely useful for liver and antioxidant capacity.

---

### Glycine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (sleep, collagen)
**Goals:** Sleep · Connective tissue · Longevity
**Technical mechanism:** Smallest amino acid; inhibitory neurotransmitter at spinal/brainstem **glycine receptors (GLRA1)**, lowers core body temperature (a sleep-onset trigger) via peripheral vasodilation, and supplies one-third of collagen's amino acids. Mimics some benefits of methionine restriction (a longevity lever).
**Molecular target:** [GLRA1 glycine receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2741) · [Glycine (PubChem CID 750)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/750)
**In plain English:** An amino acid that gently cools you down and quiets the nervous system for sleep, and is the main brick in collagen. Dirt cheap, very safe.
**Protocol:** 3–5 g before bed; 5–10 g with collagen + vitamin C for tendons.
**Bottom line:** One of the most undervalued supplements — sleep + connective tissue + longevity.
**Stacks with:** Magnesium before bed — the two together noticeably deepen sleep.
**Cost:** Very cheap — ~S$10–20/month; it’s sweet, mixes into water.

---

### CoQ10 / Ubiquinol  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (heart failure, statin users)
**Goals:** Heart · Endurance · Longevity
**Technical mechanism:** Electron carrier in mitochondrial **Complex I→III** of the electron transport chain and a lipid-phase antioxidant. Statins block the mevalonate pathway that makes both cholesterol and CoQ10, causing depletion and muscle pain.
**Molecular target:** [COQ2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/27235) · [Ubiquinol (PubChem CID 5283546)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5283546)
**In plain English:** A spark plug inside your cellular engines. Statin drugs quietly drain it, causing muscle aches — replacing it fixes that. Most valuable for statin users and heart failure.
**Protocol:** 100–300 mg with fat; ubiquinol if 40+ or on statins.
**Bottom line:** Effectively mandatory alongside statins.
**Stacks with:** Take with a fatty meal (fat-soluble); statin users benefit most, since statins deplete CoQ10.
**Cost:** ~S$25–50/month; ubiquinol absorbs better than plain ubiquinone, especially over 40.

# CATEGORY 2 — MUSCLE, STRENGTH & PERFORMANCE

---

### Beta-Alanine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Muscular endurance (60–240 s efforts)
**Technical mechanism:** Rate-limiting precursor to muscle **carnosine** (synthesised by carnosine synthase, **CARNS1**). Carnosine buffers intracellular H⁺ ions that accumulate in high-intensity work — delaying the acidosis that forces you to stop.
**Molecular target:** [CARNS1 carnosine synthase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/57571) · [Beta-alanine (PubChem CID 239)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/239)
**In plain English:** Hard effort floods muscle with acid ("the burn"). Beta-alanine builds up an acid-sponge (carnosine) so you can push through the burn longer — perfect for the 1–4 minute effort range.
**Protocol:** 3.2–6.4 g/day (split doses to reduce tingling); saturates over 4–6 weeks.
**Watch out:** Harmless skin tingling (paraesthesia).
**Bottom line:** Excellent for Hyrox-style intervals. Stacks with creatine (different energy systems).

---

### L-Citrulline / Citrulline Malate  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Endurance · Pump · Blood pressure
**Technical mechanism:** Converts to arginine → nitric oxide via endothelial **NOS3 (eNOS)**, causing vasodilation, better O₂ delivery, and reduced ammonia. More effective than oral arginine (which is destroyed first-pass).
**Molecular target:** [NOS3 endothelial nitric oxide synthase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4846) · [L-Citrulline (PubChem CID 9750)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/9750)
**In plain English:** It widens your blood vessels so more blood, oxygen, and nutrients reach working muscle — that's the "pump," plus real endurance and lower blood pressure.
**Protocol:** 6–8 g L-citrulline (or 8–12 g citrulline malate) 60 min pre-workout.
**Bottom line:** Better-studied than most pre-workout ingredients.
**Avoid:** Stacking strong blood-flow agents — citrulline + beetroot + ED drugs (PDE-5) + nitrates together can drop blood pressure dangerously.
**Cost:** ~S$20–35/month; citrulline malate is dosed higher than pure citrulline (check which you have).

---

### Betaine (Trimethylglycine)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Power · Strength · Body composition
**Technical mechanism:** Methyl donor that remethylates homocysteine to methionine (raising creatine synthesis substrate) and acts as an intracellular osmolyte (cell hydration/volumising), supporting force output.
**Molecular target:** [BHMT betaine-homocysteine methyltransferase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/635) · [Betaine (PubChem CID 247)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/247)
**In plain English:** It helps your body make more of its own creatine and swells muscle cells with water so they contract harder. Small but real strength effect.
**Protocol:** 2.5 g/day.
**Bottom line:** Modest, cheap add-on with several small positive trials.

---

### Sodium Bicarbonate  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (buffering)
**Goals:** High-intensity endurance
**Technical mechanism:** Raises blood bicarbonate, increasing the extracellular pH buffer that pulls H⁺ out of muscle — the extracellular complement to beta-alanine's intracellular buffering.
**Molecular target:** [Bicarbonate (PubChem CID 769)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/769) · [Examine: sodium bicarbonate](https://examine.com/supplements/sodium-bicarbonate/)
**In plain English:** Baking soda makes your blood better at soaking up the acid your muscles dump during hard effort, so you last longer. The catch is it can wreck your gut.
**Protocol:** 0.2–0.3 g/kg, 60–150 min pre-effort (or enteric-coated to avoid GI distress).
**Watch out:** GI upset is common — trial in training first.
**Bottom line:** Real ergogenic for repeated sprints; stomach tolerance is the limiter.

---

### HMB (β-Hydroxy β-Methylbutyrate)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (untrained/catabolic) ⭐⭐ (trained)
**Goals:** Muscle preservation
**Technical mechanism:** Leucine metabolite that stimulates muscle protein synthesis via **mTOR** and suppresses breakdown via the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway; strongest in catabolic states (caloric deficit, illness, elderly).
**Molecular target:** [MTOR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2475) · [HMB (PubChem CID 69362)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/69362)
**In plain English:** It signals "don't break down muscle." Most useful when you're dieting hard, ill, or older — much less impressive in already-trained lifters who eat enough protein.
**Protocol:** 3 g/day split.
**Bottom line:** Useful in a deficit or for the elderly; marginal for fed, trained athletes.

---

### Whey / Casein Protein  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Muscle · Recovery · Satiety
**Technical mechanism:** Complete proteins rich in **leucine**, which activates **mTORC1** to trigger muscle protein synthesis. Whey is fast-absorbing (acute MPS spike); casein is slow (overnight anti-catabolism).
**Molecular target:** [MTOR / mTORC1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2475) · [Leucine (PubChem CID 6106)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6106)
**In plain English:** Concentrated food. Leucine is the "build muscle now" trigger, and whey delivers a big hit of it. Total daily protein matters more than timing.
**Protocol:** Fill the gap to 1.6–2.2 g protein/kg bodyweight/day.
**Bottom line:** Convenience, not magic. Hit your daily protein however you like.
**Stacks with:** Creatine (add it to the same post-workout shake) and carbs.
**Cost:** ~S$50–80 per tub (~1 month). Whey isolate if lactose-sensitive.

---

### EAAs / BCAAs  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (EAAs) ⭐⭐ (BCAAs)
**Goals:** Muscle protein synthesis (fasted/low-protein)
**Technical mechanism:** Essential amino acids supply all substrate for MPS; leucine triggers mTOR. BCAAs (leu/iso/val) alone trigger the signal but lack the other bricks, so they underperform complete EAAs.
**Molecular target:** [Leucine (PubChem CID 6106)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6106) · [Examine: BCAAs](https://examine.com/supplements/bcaa/)
**In plain English:** BCAAs ring the doorbell for muscle-building but bring no building materials; full EAAs bring both. If you eat enough protein, you likely need neither.
**Protocol:** 6–12 g EAAs if training fasted or protein is low.
**Bottom line:** Redundant on an adequate-protein diet.

# CATEGORY 3 — FAT LOSS

The most disrupted category in medicine. GLP-1 drugs have made every previous fat-loss drug close to obsolete.

---

### Semaglutide (Ozempic / Wegovy / Rybelsus)  🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss · Cardiovascular risk · Type 2 diabetes
**Technical mechanism:** Long-acting agonist of the **GLP-1 receptor (GLP1R)** — a G-protein-coupled receptor in the pancreas, hypothalamic appetite centres, and vagal afferents. Activation is glucose-dependent: stimulates insulin, suppresses glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and dampens hypothalamic hunger and reward-related food cravings.
**Molecular target:** [GLP1R glucagon-like peptide 1 receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2740) · [Semaglutide (PubChem CID 56843331)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/56843331)
**In plain English:** It copies a gut hormone that tells your brain "I'm full." Hunger and constant food-thoughts fade; you eat less without willpower. STEP trials: ~15% bodyweight lost in 68 weeks.
**Protocol:** Weekly injection titrated 0.25 → 2.4 mg. Chronic — weight returns if stopped.
**Watch out:** Nausea; **major muscle loss** — resistance training + 1.6–2 g/kg protein are non-negotiable. Rare pancreatitis; thyroid C-cell black-box warning.
**Bottom line:** The most powerful weight-loss drug in history until tirzepatide/retatrutide.

---

### Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound)  🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss · Type 2 diabetes
**Technical mechanism:** Dual agonist of **GIPR (GIP receptor)** and **GLP1R**. GIP receptor activation in the hypothalamus and adipose tissue synergises with GLP-1, producing greater appetite suppression and fat loss than GLP-1 alone.
**Molecular target:** [GIPR gastric inhibitory polypeptide receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2696) · [GLP1R (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2740) · [Tirzepatide (PubChem CID 156588324)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/Tirzepatide)
**In plain English:** Hits two "I'm full" gut-hormone switches at once instead of one — so it works even better. SURMOUNT-1: 22.5% bodyweight lost, approaching bariatric-surgery territory.
**Protocol:** Weekly injection titrated 2.5 → 15 mg.
**Watch out:** Same as semaglutide; muscle-loss risk is greater given larger total loss.
**Bottom line:** Currently the most effective *approved* obesity drug.

---

### Retatrutide  🔴 (investigational — Phase 3) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss · Metabolic liver disease · Osteoarthritis pain
**Technical mechanism:** First-in-class **triple agonist** — **GLP1R + GIPR + glucagon receptor (GCGR)**. Adding glucagon-receptor agonism raises energy expenditure and hepatic fat oxidation on top of the twin appetite pathways.
**Molecular target:** [GCGR glucagon receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2642) · [GIPR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2696) · [GLP1R (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2740)
**In plain English:** Three metabolic switches in one molecule — two cut hunger, the third turns up calorie-burning. In the Phase 3 TRIUMPH-4 trial (Dec 2025) it produced **28.7% weight loss** at 68 weeks — the largest ever for a drug. Not yet approved.
**Protocol (trials):** Weekly injection to 12 mg. *Investigational — not legally available.*
**Watch out:** GI effects; glucagon arm can nudge glucose/heart rate up. Long-term safety pending.
**Bottom line:** The likely next leader once approved. *Source: [Lilly TRIUMPH-4 release](https://investor.lilly.com/news-releases/news-release-details/lillys-triple-agonist-retatrutide-delivered-weight-loss-average).*

---

### Liraglutide (Saxenda / Victoza)  🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss · Type 2 diabetes
**Technical mechanism:** Daily short-acting **GLP1R** agonist — the first-generation version of semaglutide's mechanism.
**Molecular target:** [GLP1R (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2740) · [Liraglutide (PubChem CID 44147092)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/44147092)
**In plain English:** The older, daily-injection GLP-1. Works, but weaker than weekly semaglutide and needs a daily shot.
**Protocol:** Daily injection to 3 mg.
**Bottom line:** Largely superseded by weekly agents.

---

### Orforglipron  🔴 (investigational — Phase 3) ⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss (oral)
**Technical mechanism:** Small-molecule (non-peptide) **oral GLP1R** agonist — no injection, no dietary restriction on absorption like peptide Rybelsus.
**Molecular target:** [GLP1R (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2740)
**In plain English:** A GLP-1 in a daily pill instead of a needle — the convenience breakthrough the category was waiting for. Late-stage trials ongoing.
**Bottom line:** Watch this — oral dosing could make GLP-1s far more accessible.

---

### Caffeine (thermogenic)  🟢/🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat oxidation · Energy expenditure
**Technical mechanism:** Adenosine antagonism raises catecholamines → cAMP → hormone-sensitive lipase, mobilising free fatty acids; modestly raises resting metabolic rate.
**Molecular target:** [ADORA2A (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/135) · [Caffeine (PubChem CID 2519)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2519)
**In plain English:** Nudges your body to release stored fat and burn slightly more energy. The base of nearly every fat-burner.
**Protocol:** 100–300 mg pre-training.
**Bottom line:** The only fat-burner ingredient that reliably works and is safe.

---

### Ephedrine (EC/ECA stack)  🔵/⚫ ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss · Appetite suppression
**Technical mechanism:** Non-selective adrenergic agonist and norepinephrine releaser (**ADRB1/2/3**); centrally suppresses appetite and peripherally raises thermogenesis. Synergistic with caffeine (which blocks the negative-feedback of adenosine and phosphodiesterase).
**Molecular target:** [ADRB2 beta-2 adrenergic receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/154) · [Ephedrine (PubChem CID 9294)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/9294)
**In plain English:** Flips your "fight or flight" system on a little, which curbs hunger and burns more calories. Effective but raises heart rate and blood pressure — restricted for that reason.
**Protocol:** Classic ECA = 20 mg ephedrine + 200 mg caffeine + aspirin, 2–3×/day.
**Watch out:** Hypertension, arrhythmia, anxiety. Restricted/controlled in many places.
**Bottom line:** Genuinely works; cardiovascular risk got it pulled from most shelves.

---

### Yohimbine  🟡/🔵 ⭐⭐⭐ (stubborn fat, fasted)
**Goals:** Localised/"stubborn" fat loss
**Technical mechanism:** **Alpha-2 adrenergic (ADRA2A) antagonist**. Alpha-2 receptors normally *block* fat release in "stubborn" areas (lower body); blocking them plus boosting norepinephrine unlocks lipolysis there — most effective fasted.
**Molecular target:** [ADRA2A alpha-2A adrenergic receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/150) · [Yohimbine (PubChem CID 8969)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/8969)
**In plain English:** Some fat areas have "do not burn" locks. Yohimbine picks those locks — best on an empty stomach before cardio. Can spike anxiety and heart rate.
**Protocol:** 0.2 mg/kg fasted, pre-cardio.
**Watch out:** Anxiety, palpitations, BP; avoid with caffeine sensitivity or hypertension.
**Bottom line:** Small, real effect on stubborn fat when already lean and fasted.

---

### Clenbuterol  🔴/⚫ (not approved for humans in US) ⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss · Muscle preservation
**Technical mechanism:** Long-acting **beta-2 adrenergic (ADRB2) agonist**: activates adenylate cyclase → cAMP → PKA → hormone-sensitive lipase (lipolysis) and upregulates **UCP-1** in brown fat (heat instead of ATP). Mild anti-catabolic effect on muscle.
**Molecular target:** [ADRB2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/154) · [UCP1 uncoupling protein 1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/7350) · [Clenbuterol (PubChem CID 2783)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2783)
**In plain English:** A veterinary asthma drug that cranks up fat-burning and body heat while sparing muscle. It also strains the heart, causes shakes and cramps, and can cause cardiac damage.
**Watch out:** Tachycardia, tremor, cardiac hypertrophy, hypokalemia. Banned in sport and food animals.
**Bottom line:** Effective and genuinely risky to the heart; illegal for human use in most places.

---

### 2,4-Dinitrophenol (DNP)  ⚫ / illegal ⭐⭐ (efficacy) — **DANGER**
**Goals:** Extreme fat loss *(documented for harm-reduction only — do not use)*
**Technical mechanism:** Mitochondrial **oxidative-phosphorylation uncoupler** — carries protons across the inner membrane, so the proton gradient is dissipated as heat instead of making ATP. The body burns enormous energy to compensate (~+17% expenditure in animals).
**Molecular target:** [2,4-Dinitrophenol (PubChem CID 1493)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/1493) · [DNP toxicity review (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3550200/)
**In plain English:** It makes your cellular engines run hot and wasteful, so you melt fat — but the same mechanism can literally cook you from the inside. The lethal dose is close to the effective dose. People die from it every year.
**Watch out:** Fatal hyperthermia, no antidote, narrow margin. **Illegal; documented here only so the danger is unambiguous.**
**Bottom line:** Do not use. Included for completeness and to state plainly that it kills.

---

### Berberine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (glucose) ⭐⭐⭐ (weight)
**Goals:** Fat loss · Glucose · Lipids · Gut
**Technical mechanism:** Activates **AMPK** by the same energy-sensor route as [metformin](/c/metformin). *The AMPK switch → [the AMPK pathway](/pathway/3).* Downstream: lowers hepatic glucose output, improves insulin sensitivity, shifts fuel toward fat oxidation, and reshapes gut microbiota. Its real weakness is **poor oral bioavailability** — dihydroberberine absorbs far better.
**Molecular target:** [PRKAA1 AMPK catalytic subunit (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5562) · [Berberine (PubChem CID 2353)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2353)
**In plain English:** "Nature's metformin" — it flips the same low-fuel switch ([AMPK](/pathway/3)). Best for pre-diabetes and high triglycerides. Poorly absorbed, so split the dose with meals or use dihydroberberine.
**Protocol:** 500 mg 3×/day with meals; dihydroberberine for better absorption.
**Watch out:** GI upset early; CYP3A4 inhibitor (drug interactions).
**Bottom line:** Strong metabolic supplement; pair with glucose monitoring.
**Avoid:** Other blood-sugar-lowering drugs (metformin, insulin) without monitoring — additive hypoglycaemia. It inhibits CYP3A4, so it interacts with many prescriptions (statins, immunosuppressants, some blood thinners) — check with a pharmacist first.
**Cost:** ~S$25–45/month; dihydroberberine costs more but absorbs far better.

---

### Green Tea Extract (EGCG)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (modest)
**Goals:** Fat oxidation
**Technical mechanism:** EGCG inhibits **catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT)**, prolonging norepinephrine's fat-mobilising signal; synergistic with caffeine.
**Molecular target:** [COMT (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1312) · [EGCG (PubChem CID 65064)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/65064)
**In plain English:** Makes your natural fat-burning signal last longer. Real but small; watch high-dose liver effects.
**Protocol:** 400–500 mg EGCG/day.
**Watch out:** Rare liver toxicity at high isolated doses — take with food.
**Bottom line:** Minor helper, not a driver.

---

### Naltrexone/Bupropion (Contrave)  🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fat loss (appetite/craving)
**Technical mechanism:** Bupropion stimulates hypothalamic **POMC** neurons (dopamine/norepinephrine reuptake inhibition); naltrexone (**opioid µ-receptor, OPRM1** antagonist) blocks POMC's auto-inhibition — together sustaining appetite/craving suppression.
**Molecular target:** [OPRM1 mu-opioid receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4988) · [Naltrexone (PubChem CID 5360515)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5360515)
**In plain English:** Targets the brain's reward/craving circuit rather than gut fullness — useful for emotional or reward eating. Modest (~5–9%) loss.
**Bottom line:** Reasonable non-GLP-1 option, especially for craving-driven eating.

---

### Phentermine / Qsymia (phentermine-topiramate)  🟢/⚫ ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Short-term (phentermine) / longer-term (Qsymia) fat loss
**Technical mechanism:** Phentermine is a norepinephrine-releasing appetite suppressant (amphetamine relative, Schedule IV). Topiramate adds appetite suppression via GABA modulation and carbonic-anhydrase inhibition.
**Molecular target:** [Phentermine (PubChem CID 4771)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/4771) · [SLC6A2 norepinephrine transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6530)
**In plain English:** Turns down appetite through the adrenaline system. Phentermine alone is short-term; the Qsymia combo works longer. Pre-GLP-1 mainstays.
**Bottom line:** Effective, older prescription options now second-line to GLP-1s.

---

# CATEGORY 4 — HORMONAL HEALTH & TESTOSTERONE

The whole androgen axis and its support drugs. Every anabolic works through the same receptor — the androgen receptor.

---

### Testosterone (TRT)  ⚫/🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Muscle · Fat loss · Libido · Mood · Bone
**Technical mechanism:** Binds the cytoplasmic **androgen receptor (AR)**; the hormone-receptor complex translocates to the nucleus and binds **androgen response elements (AREs)** on DNA, switching on genes for muscle protein synthesis, erythropoiesis, and bone density. Amplified in some tissues by 5α-reductase (→ DHT) and aromatised to estradiol by **CYP19A1**.
**Molecular target:** [AR androgen receptor (NCBI Gene 367)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367) · [AR — GeneCards](https://www.genecards.org/cgi-bin/carddisp.pl?gene=AR) · [Testosterone (PubChem CID 6013)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6013)
**In plain English:** Testosterone slots into a docking station (the androgen receptor) inside your cells, which then walks to the cell's DNA and switches on the "build muscle, make blood, strengthen bone" genes. TRT restores a *normal* level in men who are genuinely low — not a super-dose.
**Protocol (Rx):** Weekly testosterone cypionate/enanthate injection; monitor total/free T, estradiol, hematocrit, PSA.
**Watch out:** Testicular shrinkage/infertility (suppresses LH/FSH), high hematocrit (clot risk), estradiol rise. Schedule III.
**Bottom line:** Transformative for confirmed low-T; a different risk equation for enhancement in normal men.

---

### HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)  🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Fertility / testicular preservation on TRT
**Technical mechanism:** Mimics **luteinising hormone (LH)** at the **LHCGR** receptor on testicular Leydig cells, driving intratesticular testosterone and sperm production — bypassing the pituitary shutdown that TRT causes.
**Molecular target:** [LHCGR LH/hCG receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3973) · [Choriogonadotropin (PubChem)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/Human-chorionic-gonadotropin)
**In plain English:** TRT tells the testicles to switch off, so they shrink and fertility drops. HCG impersonates the brain's "keep working" signal so they stay online.
**Bottom line:** Standard companion to TRT for men wanting to preserve fertility/size.

---

### Enclomiphene / Clomiphene (Clomid)  🔵/🟠 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Raise natural testosterone · Fertility · Post-cycle recovery
**Technical mechanism:** Selective estrogen receptor modulator (**SERM**) — blocks **estrogen receptor (ESR1)** at the hypothalamus, removing estrogen's negative feedback so the brain releases more LH/FSH, raising the body's *own* testosterone. Enclomiphene is the purified active isomer.
**Molecular target:** [ESR1 estrogen receptor alpha (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2099) · [Clomifene (PubChem CID 2800)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2800)
**In plain English:** Tricks the brain into thinking estrogen is low, so it revs up its own testosterone production — raising T without shutting the testicles down like TRT does.
**Bottom line:** Good option for younger men who want higher T while keeping fertility.

---

### Anastrozole / Exemestane (Aromatase Inhibitors)  🔵/🟠 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Control estrogen on TRT/cycle
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibit **aromatase (CYP19A1)**, the enzyme converting testosterone to estradiol. Anastrozole is reversible; exemestane is a suicide (irreversible) inhibitor.
**Molecular target:** [CYP19A1 aromatase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1588) · [Anastrozole (PubChem CID 2187)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2187)
**In plain English:** High testosterone partly converts to estrogen, which can cause bloating and gyno. These drugs block that conversion. Easy to overdo — crashing estrogen wrecks mood, joints, and libido.
**Bottom line:** Use only if estradiol is genuinely high; low estrogen is as bad as high.

---

### Tamoxifen (Nolvadex)  🔵/🟠 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Gynecomastia prevention · Post-cycle therapy
**Technical mechanism:** SERM — antagonist at breast-tissue **ESR1** (blocks gyno) while agonising it at the hypothalamus (restarts LH/FSH after a cycle).
**Molecular target:** [ESR1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2099) · [Tamoxifen (PubChem CID 2733526)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/2733526)
**In plain English:** Blocks estrogen at the chest to stop breast-tissue growth, and kick-starts the body's hormone system back on after a steroid cycle.
**Bottom line:** Core post-cycle-therapy and anti-gyno drug.

---

### DHEA  🟡/🔵 ⭐⭐⭐ (aging) ⭐ (young men)
**Goals:** Hormonal support (50+) · Bone
**Technical mechanism:** Adrenal precursor upstream of both testosterone and estradiol; declines ~80% by age 70. Supplementation raises downstream hormones mainly in older adults and women.
**Molecular target:** [DHEA (PubChem CID 5881)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5881) · [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367)
**In plain English:** A raw material your body turns into sex hormones. Refilling it helps older people whose levels crashed; does little for young men who already have plenty.
**Protocol:** 25–50 mg (men 40+), 10–25 mg (women).
**Watch out:** Androgenic effects in women; WADA-banned; caution in hormone-sensitive cancers.
**Bottom line:** For the 50+ with low DHEA-S, not young athletes.

---

### Finasteride / Dutasteride  🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Hair retention · Prostate (BPH) — *anti-androgen in scalp/prostate*
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibit **5α-reductase (SRD5A2)**, blocking testosterone→DHT conversion. DHT drives male-pattern hair loss and prostate growth. Finasteride hits type II; dutasteride hits I and II.
**Molecular target:** [SRD5A2 5-alpha reductase 2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6716) · [Finasteride (PubChem CID 57363)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/57363)
**In plain English:** Testosterone gets converted into a stronger cousin (DHT) that kills scalp follicles. These drugs block that conversion to save hair — but for a minority, lowering DHT dents libido/mood ("post-finasteride" concerns).
**Protocol:** Finasteride 1 mg/day (hair), 5 mg (prostate).
**Watch out:** Sexual side effects in a minority, sometimes persistent; not for women of childbearing age.
**Bottom line:** Most effective hair-loss drug; weigh the sexual-side-effect risk honestly.

---

### Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Free testosterone · Libido · Stress
**Technical mechanism:** Eurycomanone quassinoids lower **SHBG** (freeing bound testosterone) and reduce cortisol, raising the free-T:cortisol ratio — most in stressed men.
**Molecular target:** [SHBG (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6462) · [Eurycomanone (PubChem CID 10547207)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/10547207)
**In plain English:** Frees up testosterone that's chemically "handcuffed," and lowers stress hormone — so more of your own T is usable. Best evidence of the herbal T-boosters.
**Protocol:** 200–400 mg standardised (Physta/LJ100).
**Watch out:** Heavy-metal contamination in poor-quality sources.
**Bottom line:** The most credible herbal testosterone support.
**Avoid:** Hormone-sensitive conditions — it can raise testosterone; get advice if you have prostate issues.
**Cost:** ~S$25–45/month; look for a standardised extract (Physta / ~1.5% eurycomanone), not raw powder.

---

### Fadogia Agrestis  🟡 ⭐ (rat data only)
**Goals:** Testosterone *(hype > evidence)*
**Technical mechanism:** Proposed LH-mimetic/Leydig-cell stimulation — but only in rodents, where the same studies also show dose-dependent **testicular toxicity**.
**Molecular target:** [Examine: Fadogia agrestis](https://examine.com/supplements/fadogia-agrestis/)
**In plain English:** Podcast-famous, but there are zero human studies and the rat studies show it can damage the testicles. Zinc, D3, boron and tongkat do the same job with real human data and no toxicity signal.
**Bottom line:** Avoid until human safety data exists.

---

# CATEGORY 5 — ANABOLIC-ANDROGENIC STEROIDS (AAS)

⚫ **All Schedule III controlled substances (US); illegal without prescription.** Documented for completeness and harm-reduction. Every one works through the **androgen receptor (AR)** — they differ in anabolic:androgenic ratio, aromatisation, and liver toxicity.
*General mechanism: [AR androgen receptor (NCBI Gene 367)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367). Structural review: [AAS receptor signalling (MDPI 2026)](https://www.mdpi.com/1422-0067/27/6/2581).*

---

### Nandrolone (Deca-Durabolin)  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐ (efficacy)
**Technical mechanism:** 19-nortestosterone; high anabolic:androgenic ratio, converts to weaker DHN not DHT (mild on scalp/prostate) but to nandrolone-estrogen and progesterone activity. Notably improves collagen synthesis and joint comfort.
**Molecular target:** [Nandrolone (PubChem CID 9904)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/9904) · [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367)
**In plain English:** "Deca." Builds muscle steadily and eases joints, but its progesterone-like activity causes the classic "deca dick" libido crash and long detection window.
**Watch out:** Sexual dysfunction, prolonged HPTA suppression.

---

### Trenbolone  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐ (efficacy) — high harm
**Technical mechanism:** 19-nor with extreme AR binding affinity (higher than testosterone), does not aromatise, strong nutrient-partitioning and lipolysis; also binds glucocorticoid receptor.
**Molecular target:** [Trenbolone (PubChem CID 25015)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/25015) · [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367)
**In plain English:** The most powerful and most punishing common steroid — dramatic muscle and fat changes alongside night sweats, insomnia, aggression, cardiovascular strain, and "tren cough."
**Watch out:** Severe cardiovascular/neurological/psychological effects. Among the highest-harm AAS.

---

### Oxandrolone (Anavar)  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Mild DHT-derived oral, 17α-alkylated (liver-resistant but still hepatic), low androgenicity, no aromatisation; favoured for lean gains and by women.
**Molecular target:** [Oxandrolone (PubChem CID 5878)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5878) · [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367)
**In plain English:** "Anavar." The "mild" oral — modest lean strength gains, popular with women, but still suppresses natural hormones and stresses the liver and cholesterol.
**Watch out:** Lipid deterioration, HPTA suppression.

---

### Stanozolol (Winstrol)  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** DHT-derived, 17α-alkylated, non-aromatising; lowers SHBG sharply (raising free hormone), dry/hard look.
**Molecular target:** [Stanozolol (PubChem CID 25249)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/25249) · [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367)
**In plain English:** "Winny." Gives a hard, dry look for shows — at the cost of painful joints (dries them out), bad cholesterol, and liver strain.
**Watch out:** Joint pain, hepatotoxicity, tendon injury risk.

---

### Boldenone (Equipoise)  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Testosterone with a 1,2 double bond; slow, steady anabolic, mild aromatisation, raises RBC/appetite.
**Molecular target:** [Boldenone (PubChem CID 6537)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6537)
**In plain English:** "EQ." Slow, lean gains and big appetite/endurance boost from higher red blood cells — but that same RBC rise thickens blood.
**Watch out:** Polycythemia, anxiety in some.

---

### Methandrostenolone (Dianabol)  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Oral 17α-alkylated, aromatises readily; rapid mass/strength via glycogen and protein synthesis.
**Molecular target:** [Metandienone (PubChem CID 6300)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6300)
**In plain English:** "Dbol." Fast, dramatic size and strength (much of it water) — with high blood pressure, estrogen, and liver load. The classic bulk kick-starter.
**Watch out:** Hepatotoxic, hypertension, gyno.

---

### Oxymetholone (Anadrol) · Drostanolone (Masteron) · Methenolone (Primobolan)  ⚫ ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Anadrol — potent oral for mass (non-AR estrogenic effects, heavy liver load). Masteron — DHT-derived, anti-estrogenic "dry/hard" cosmetic. Primobolan — mild, low-side-effect DHT-derived, prized for lean quality.
**Molecular target:** [Oxymetholone (PubChem CID 5877)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5877) · [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367)
**In plain English:** Anadrol = maximum size fast and hardest on the liver; Masteron = cosmetic hardening for lean physiques; Primobolan = the "gentle" injectable people run for clean, keepable muscle.
**Watch out:** Anadrol hepatotoxicity; all suppress natural testosterone.

# CATEGORY 6 — SARMs & SELECTIVE METABOLIC AGENTS

🔴 **None are FDA-approved; all are banned in sport (WADA/USADA) and sold as "research chemicals."** SARMs aim to activate the androgen receptor selectively in muscle/bone while sparing prostate and scalp.
*General mechanism: [AR androgen receptor (NCBI Gene 367)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367). When a SARM binds AR, heat-shock proteins release, the complex enters the nucleus and binds AREs to drive muscle/bone gene expression — with tissue selectivity testosterone lacks.*

---

### Ostarine (MK-2866, Enobosarm)  🔴 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Moderate-potency non-steroidal AR agonist selective for muscle and bone; the most clinically studied SARM (cancer-cachexia trials).
**Molecular target:** [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367) · [Enobosarm (PubChem CID 11326715)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/11326715)
**In plain English:** The "beginner" SARM — mild muscle/bone gains with fewer steroid-like side effects, but still suppresses your own testosterone and can raise liver enzymes.
**Watch out:** HPTA suppression, liver injury cases, positive drug tests.

---

### Ligandrol (LGD-4033)  🔴 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** High-affinity, high-potency AR agonist (>500:1 anabolic:androgenic in models), strong muscle selectivity, oral.
**Molecular target:** [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367) · [LGD-4033 (PubChem CID 44137675)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/44137675)
**In plain English:** One of the strongest SARMs for size — and correspondingly strong at shutting down your natural testosterone. Liver-injury reports exist.
**Watch out:** Marked suppression, hepatotoxicity.

---

### Testolone (RAD-140)  🔴 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Potent AR agonist designed to mimic testosterone's anabolism with lower prostate activity; created for muscle-wasting and breast cancer research.
**Molecular target:** [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367) · [RAD-140 (PubChem CID 118862653)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/RAD140)
**In plain English:** Among the most powerful SARMs. Real muscle gains, but case reports include liver injury and even myocarditis (heart-muscle inflammation).
**Watch out:** Hepatotoxicity, documented cardiac case reports.

---

### Andarine (S4) · YK-11 · S-23  🔴 ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Andarine — mild AR agonist, causes yellow-vision (binds retinal receptors). YK-11 — AR agonist *and* myostatin/follistatin modulator (removes a brake on muscle growth). S-23 — high-potency, strongly suppressive, studied as a male contraceptive.
**Molecular target:** [AR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/367) · [YK-11 (PubChem CID 44224998)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/44224998)
**In plain English:** The more experimental SARMs — YK-11 also releases the "myostatin" brake that limits muscle, S-23 is the strongest and most suppressive, Andarine can literally tint your vision yellow.
**Watch out:** Minimal human data, strong suppression, vision effects (S4).

---

### Cardarine (GW-501516)  🔴 ⭐⭐ — **cancer signal**
**Technical mechanism:** Not a SARM — a **PPARδ (PPARD) agonist**. Switches muscle fuel use toward fat oxidation and boosts endurance/mitochondrial genes; an "exercise mimetic."
**Molecular target:** [PPARD peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor delta (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5467) · [GW501516 (PubChem CID 9803963)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/9803963)
**In plain English:** Tells muscles to burn fat for fuel and dramatically boosts endurance — but development was **halted because it caused cancer in multiple organs in rodent studies at multiple doses.**
**Watch out:** Dose- and multi-site carcinogenicity in animals. This is the key reason it was abandoned.

---

### Stenabolic (SR9009)  🔴 ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** **REV-ERBα (NR1D1) agonist** — a circadian-clock nuclear receptor; increases mitochondrial number and metabolic rate ("exercise mimetic"). Very poor oral bioavailability in humans.
**Molecular target:** [NR1D1 REV-ERB alpha (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/9572) · [SR9009 (PubChem CID 57394020)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/57394020)
**In plain English:** Works on the body-clock gene to make more cellular engines and burn more energy — impressive in mice, but it's barely absorbed as a pill, so human benefit is doubtful.
**Bottom line:** Interesting biology, weak real-world human case.

# CATEGORY 7 — GROWTH HORMONE AXIS PEPTIDES

These raise growth hormone (GH) and IGF-1. Two families: **GHRH analogs** (act on the GHRH receptor, GHRHR) and **ghrelin mimetics / GHS** (act on the growth-hormone-secretagogue receptor, GHSR). Combining one of each produces a bigger, more natural GH pulse than either alone. Most are 🔴 not approved; **Tesamorelin is the one FDA-approved GH secretagogue.**

---

### Tesamorelin (Egrifta)  🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Visceral fat loss · GH/IGF-1 · (studied for cognition)
**Technical mechanism:** Stabilised 44-amino-acid **GHRH analog** binding the pituitary **GHRHR**, driving pulsatile GH → hepatic IGF-1. FDA-approved to reduce visceral adipose tissue in HIV-associated lipodystrophy.
**Molecular target:** [GHRHR GHRH receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2692) · [Tesamorelin (PubChem CID 16137828)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/16137828)
**In plain English:** A refined copy of the brain's "release growth hormone" signal. Uniquely proven to shrink deep belly fat, and the only GH-releasing peptide with an actual FDA approval.
**Protocol (Rx):** ~2 mg daily subcutaneous.
**Watch out:** Raises IGF-1 (caution with cancer risk), can worsen glucose.
**Bottom line:** The legitimate, approved member of this family.

---

### Sermorelin  🔴/🔵(compounded) ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Short GHRH(1-29) fragment binding **GHRHR**; shortest-acting, most "physiological" GH nudge.
**Molecular target:** [GHRHR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2692) · [Sermorelin (PubChem CID 16132300)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/16132300)
**In plain English:** The gentle, old-school GH releaser — a brief tap on the "make growth hormone" button, often prescribed by anti-aging clinics.
**Bottom line:** Mild; frequently compounded for "anti-aging" GH support.

---

### CJC-1295 (± DAC)  🔴 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Long-acting **GHRHR** agonist; the DAC (drug-affinity complex) version binds albumin for a multi-day half-life, giving a sustained GH "bleed" rather than a pulse.
**Molecular target:** [GHRHR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2692) · [CJC-1295 (PubChem CID 91976842)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/CJC-1295)
**In plain English:** A longer-lasting GH releaser. Usually paired with ipamorelin so one raises the "how much" and the other the "release now."
**Bottom line:** Common half of the "CJC + ipamorelin" stack.

---

### Ipamorelin  🔴 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Selective **GHSR (ghrelin receptor)** agonist (pentapeptide) that triggers GH release **without** the cortisol, prolactin, or hunger spikes of older GHRPs.
**Molecular target:** [GHSR growth hormone secretagogue receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2693) · [Ipamorelin (PubChem CID 9831659)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/9831659)
**In plain English:** Presses the "release growth hormone now" button cleanly — without making you ravenous or spiking stress hormones like the older versions did.
**Bottom line:** The "clean" ghrelin-side peptide; standard partner to CJC-1295.

---

### Hexarelin / GHRP-2 / GHRP-6  🔴 ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Older **GHSR** agonists; strong GH release but also raise cortisol/prolactin (GHRP-2) and hunger (GHRP-6); hexarelin is most potent but desensitises fastest.
**Molecular target:** [GHSR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2693) · [GHRP-6 (PubChem CID 9938226)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/GHRP-6)
**In plain English:** First-generation ghrelin peptides — powerful GH release but with side baggage (hunger, stress hormones) that ipamorelin was designed to fix.
**Bottom line:** Largely replaced by ipamorelin.

---

### MK-677 (Ibutamoren)  🔴 ⭐⭐⭐
**Goals:** Muscle · Recovery · Deep sleep · Appetite
**Technical mechanism:** Orally-active non-peptide **GHSR** agonist; sustained GH/IGF-1 elevation and marked slow-wave-sleep enhancement (GH pulses in deep sleep). Reversed nitrogen wasting in trials.
**Molecular target:** [GHSR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2693) · [Ibutamoren (PubChem CID 636866)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/636866)
**In plain English:** A pill (not an injection) that keeps growth hormone elevated via the ghrelin switch — famous for deep sleep and appetite, plus fuller muscles. Downsides are water retention and higher blood sugar.
**Protocol:** 10–25 mg at night.
**Watch out:** Raised fasting glucose, water retention, joint aches; not for diabetics or active cancer.
**Bottom line:** The most practical GH-optimiser without a needle.

---

### Human Growth Hormone (Somatropin)  🔵/⚫ ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (deficiency)
**Technical mechanism:** Recombinant GH acts directly on the **GH receptor (GHR)** → JAK2/STAT5 signalling → hepatic **IGF-1**, driving lipolysis, protein synthesis, and collagen turnover.
**Molecular target:** [GHR growth hormone receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2690) · [Somatropin (PubChem)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/Somatropin)
**In plain English:** The actual hormone, injected — rather than coaxing your pituitary to make more. Powerful for genuine deficiency; misused for physique it carries real metabolic and growth risks.
**Watch out:** Insulin resistance, edema, carpal tunnel, acromegaly features, possible tumour growth. Controlled/prescription.
**Bottom line:** Legitimate for diagnosed deficiency; high-risk off-label.

---

### IGF-1 LR3  🔴 ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Long-acting insulin-like growth factor-1 analog binding **IGF1R**, driving hyperplasia (new muscle cells) and protein synthesis; LR3 modification evades IGF-binding proteins for extended action.
**Molecular target:** [IGF1R IGF-1 receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3480) · [IGF-1 (PubChem)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/IGF-1)
**In plain English:** The growth factor GH actually works through, injected directly. Potent muscle-growth signal — but IGF-1 also feeds tumours and drops blood sugar dangerously.
**Watch out:** Hypoglycemia, cancer-growth concern, organ growth.
**Bottom line:** High potency, high risk, no human safety data for this use.

---

### Follistatin / Myostatin inhibitors (ACE-031, YK-11)  🔴 ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Block **myostatin (MSTN/GDF-8)** — the body's natural brake on muscle size. Removing the brake (as in "double-muscled" animals and rare human mutations) permits large muscle growth.
**Molecular target:** [MSTN myostatin/GDF8 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2660) · [Follistatin FST (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/10468)
**In plain English:** Your body caps muscle growth with a protein called myostatin. These release that cap — the same reason certain super-muscular cattle and dogs exist. Human safety is unknown; ACE-031 trials were stopped for side effects (nosebleeds, vessel changes).
**Watch out:** Off-target vascular/bleeding effects; unknown long-term safety.
**Bottom line:** Compelling mechanism, unproven and risky in humans.

# CATEGORY 8 — HEALING & RECOVERY PEPTIDES

🔴 **Not FDA-approved.** Note the **April 2026 reclassification**: BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, KPV and others were removed from FDA Category 2, and BPC-157/TB-500/KPV face a July 23–24 2026 PCAC compounding review. Removal ≠ approval — these remain legally grey and are sold "for research."

---

### BPC-157  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026; PCAC review Jul 2026) ⭐⭐⭐ (animal) ⭐ (human)
**Goals:** Tendon/ligament/muscle healing · Gut · Joints
**Technical mechanism:** A 15-amino-acid fragment of a gastric protein. Two characterised pathways: (1) upregulates **VEGFR2 (KDR)** → PI3K/Akt → **eNOS (NOS3)**, driving angiogenesis (new blood vessels to injured tissue); (2) a **Src–Caveolin-1–eNOS** route. It also modulates the nitric-oxide system and growth-hormone-receptor expression in tendon fibroblasts.
**Molecular target:** [KDR/VEGFR2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3791) · [NOS3 eNOS (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4846) · [Mechanism paper (Nature Sci Reports)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-020-74022-y) · [Narrative review (PMC 2025)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12446177/)
**In plain English:** It tells injured tissue to grow a fresh blood supply (via the VEGFR2 switch), and blood flow is what healing runs on — so tendons, ligaments, gut lining and muscle appear to mend faster. The catch: almost all the evidence is in rats. Human trials essentially don't exist yet, which is why the FDA status is unsettled.
**Protocol (research):** 200–500 mcg/day, subcutaneous near injury or oral (gut).
**Watch out:** No human safety data; theoretical tumour-angiogenesis concern; unregulated product purity.
**Bottom line:** The most promising healing peptide by animal data — and the biggest human-evidence gap. Watch the July 2026 PCAC decision.

---

### TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 fragment)  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026) ⭐⭐ (animal)
**Goals:** Systemic tissue repair · Inflammation
**Technical mechanism:** Fragment of **thymosin β4 (TMSB4X)**; regulates **actin** polymerisation to enable cell migration to wounds, promotes angiogenesis, and reduces inflammation. Studied in cardiac and corneal repair models.
**Molecular target:** [TMSB4X thymosin beta 4 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/7114) · [TB-500 (PubChem)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/Thymosin-beta-4)
**In plain English:** Helps repair cells physically crawl to the site of damage and calms inflammation — a more "whole-body" healer than BPC-157's local action. Usually stacked with BPC-157. Human data is anecdotal.
**Protocol (research):** 2–5 mg 2×/week.
**Bottom line:** Plausible from animal work; no human trials. Commonly paired with BPC-157.

---

### GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)  🔴 (topical OTC in cosmetics) ⭐⭐⭐ (skin) ⭐⭐ (systemic)
**Goals:** Skin/collagen · Wound healing · Anti-aging
**Technical mechanism:** Copper-binding tripeptide that, remarkably, **resets gene expression** — a 2010 analysis showed GHK-Cu shifts ~4,000 human genes toward a younger profile, upregulating collagen, decorin, and antioxidant/DNA-repair genes and modulating **TGF-β** and metalloproteinases.
**Molecular target:** [GHK-Cu genomic review (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3402088/) · [GHK tripeptide (PubChem CID 73587880)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/73587880)
**In plain English:** A copper-carrying peptide that seems to nudge thousands of genes back toward a "younger" setting — most proven for skin (collagen, wound healing). The strongest independent evidence of the cosmetic peptides; topical forms are widely sold.
**Protocol:** Topical serums (OTC); injectable is the reclassified/grey form.
**Bottom line:** Best-evidenced anti-aging peptide for skin; systemic claims less proven.

---

### KPV  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026; PCAC review Jul 2026) ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** C-terminal tripeptide of α-MSH; anti-inflammatory via **melanocortin/NF-κB** suppression in gut and skin — studied for IBD and wound healing.
**Molecular target:** [Alpha-MSH/POMC (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5443) · [KPV (PubChem)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/KPV)
**In plain English:** A tiny anti-inflammatory peptide, mostly explored for gut inflammation and skin. Early data only.
**Bottom line:** Promising gut/skin anti-inflammatory; limited human evidence.

---

### Thymosin Alpha-1 (Zadaxin)  🔵(approved abroad)/🔴(US) ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Immunomodulator activating **Toll-like receptors (TLR9/2)** and T-cell maturation; approved in ~35 countries for hepatitis B/C and as a vaccine adjuvant.
**Molecular target:** [TLR9 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/54106) · [Thymalfasin (PubChem)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/Thymalfasin)
**In plain English:** Tunes up the immune system's T-cells — approved in many countries (not the US) for chronic infections and used to bolster immunity.
**Bottom line:** Real immunotherapy abroad; investigational in the US.

---

### LL-37  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026) ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Human cathelicidin (**CAMP** gene) antimicrobial peptide; disrupts microbial membranes and modulates immunity/wound healing.
**Molecular target:** [CAMP cathelicidin (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/820) · [Frontiers review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC13095733/)
**In plain English:** A natural antibiotic your own body makes; explored for infections and healing. Very early for supplemental use.
**Bottom line:** Interesting antimicrobial biology; minimal human supplement data.

# CATEGORY 9 — LONGEVITY & HEALTHSPAN

The most active frontier in medicine. Most are repurposed metabolic or cancer drugs targeting the "hallmarks of aging." Animal data is often strong; human lifespan data is necessarily incomplete.

---

### Rapamycin (Sirolimus)  🟠 (off-label) / 🟢 (transplant) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (animal) ⭐⭐⭐ (human, emerging)
**Goals:** Longevity · Immune rejuvenation
**Technical mechanism:** Binds FKBP12 to inhibit **mTOR complex 1** — flipping cells from growth to maintenance (autophagy, stress resistance, less senescence). *What mTOR is and why dialling it down matters → [the mTOR pathway](/pathway/2).* The compound-specific nuance is **intermittent** dosing: weekly dosing inhibits mTORC1 while sparing **mTORC2**, avoiding the insulin resistance daily dosing causes.
**Molecular target:** [MTOR mechanistic target of rapamycin (NCBI Gene 2475)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2475) · [Sirolimus (PubChem CID 5284616)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5284616)
**In plain English:** Rapamycin dials the "grow" switch ([mTOR](/pathway/2)) *down* periodically, flipping cells into "clean and repair." It extends lifespan in every animal tested — the single most reliable longevity drug — but human data is still coming in. Taken weekly, not daily.
**Protocol (off-label):** ~5–6 mg once weekly (intermittent), physician-monitored.
**Watch out:** Immunosuppression (dose-dependent), mouth sores, raised lipids/glucose. Not a self-prescription.
**Bottom line:** The most compelling longevity drug we have; still needs human RCTs (PEARL, others ongoing).

---

### Metformin  🟠 (longevity) / 🟢 (T2DM) ⭐⭐⭐ (longevity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (diabetes)
**Goals:** Longevity · Glucose · Metabolic health
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibits mitochondrial **Complex I**, raising the AMP:ATP ratio → activates **AMPK**. *How the AMPK "burn, don't hoard" switch works → [the AMPK pathway](/pathway/3).* On top of that it lowers hepatic gluconeogenesis, improves insulin sensitivity and reshapes the gut microbiome. The **TAME** trial is the first FDA-sanctioned trial targeting ageing itself.
**Molecular target:** [PRKAA1 AMPK (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5562) · [Metformin (PubChem CID 4091)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/4091)
**In plain English:** Metformin trips the same low-fuel switch ([AMPK](/pathway/3)) that exercise does. Diabetics on it have outlived non-diabetics in some data. The catch for athletes: because it partly *duplicates* the exercise signal, it can blunt muscle and fitness gains from hard training.
**Protocol (off-label):** 500–1,000 mg/day; add B12.
**Watch out:** GI upset (use ER), blunts exercise adaptation, B12 depletion.
**Bottom line:** Best for the sedentary/metabolically-at-risk; may cost gains if you train hard.

---

### Acarbose  🟠 (longevity) / 🟢 (T2DM) ⭐⭐⭐ (animal) 
**Goals:** Longevity · Post-meal glucose
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibits intestinal **α-glucosidase (MGAM/SI)**, slowing carbohydrate digestion and blunting post-meal glucose spikes — mimicking mild caloric restriction. Extended median lifespan in the NIA Interventions Testing Program, especially in males.
**Molecular target:** [MGAM maltase-glucoamylase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/8972) · [Acarbose (PubChem CID 41774)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/41774)
**In plain English:** Blocks part of carb digestion so sugar trickles in instead of spiking — like a partial low-carb effect in a pill. One of the more reproducible lifespan-extenders in mice.
**Watch out:** Gas/bloating (undigested carbs ferment).
**Bottom line:** Cheap, prescription, solid animal longevity data.

---

### NMN / NR (NAD⁺ precursors)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (metabolic) ⭐⭐ (performance)
**Goals:** Longevity · Energy metabolism · Endurance (40+)
**Technical mechanism:** Refill cellular **NAD⁺** — the fuel your sirtuins (SIRT1) and PARP DNA-repair enzymes run on. *Why NAD⁺ matters and how it halves with age → [the NAD⁺ &amp; sirtuins pathway](/pathway/12).* The forms differ only in how they reach NAD⁺: **NR** converts to NMN inside the cell; **NMN** is thought to enter via the **Slc12a8** transporter; **niacin** is the cheap classic precursor; a **NAD⁺ IV** infuses it directly.
**Molecular target:** [NAMPT NAD biosynthesis (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/10135) · [SIRT1 sirtuin 1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/23411) · [NMN (PubChem CID 14180)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/14180)
**In plain English:** Age drains NAD⁺; these refill it. Which form? **NR** — cleanest legal status, most human data. **NMN** — the most-hyped, newer, a US legal grey area. **Niacin (B3)** — cheapest, but flushes. **NAD⁺ drip** — fastest and priciest, least evidence per dollar, clinic-only. Animal data is spectacular; human benefits look real but modest, and matter more after 40.
**Protocol:** NMN or NR 250–500 mg in the **morning** — NAD⁺ tracks your body clock and peaks in the active phase. Often stacked with pterostilbene, and with **TMG** to replace the methyl groups NAD⁺ metabolism uses up.
**Watch out:** NMN is a US legal grey area (FDA drug-exclusion ruling); **NR is the clean supplement.** No prescription drug is involved — but skip NAD⁺ IVs from unlicensed providers.
**Bottom line:** The most exciting longevity lever right now; NR the cleaner legal status, NMN the newer trials, niacin the best value. Full mechanism lives on [the NAD⁺ pathway](/pathway/12).
**Stacks with:** Pterostilbene (sirtuin activation from another angle) and TMG / betaine (replaces the methyl groups NAD⁺ metabolism uses up).
**Cost:** ~S$40–90/month for NMN; NR (Niagen) is pricier. On iHerb / Shopee — verify third-party testing, purity varies widely.

---

### Resveratrol / Pterostilbene  🟡 ⭐⭐ (resveratrol) ⭐⭐ (pterostilbene)
**Technical mechanism:** Polyphenol **SIRT1** activators (sirtuin pathway, overlaps caloric-restriction signalling). Pterostilbene is the dimethylated form — far better bioavailability and half-life.
**Molecular target:** [SIRT1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/23411) · [Resveratrol (PubChem CID 445154)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/445154)
**In plain English:** The "red wine molecule" and its more absorbable cousin flip on the same longevity genes (sirtuins) that fasting does. Human results have been underwhelming for resveratrol (poor absorption); pterostilbene absorbs much better.
**Protocol:** Pterostilbene 100–250 mg; often paired with NMN/NR.
**Bottom line:** Weak human evidence; pterostilbene the better-absorbed choice.

---

### Fisetin  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (animal senolytic)
**Goals:** Longevity (clearing senescent cells)
**Technical mechanism:** Flavonoid **senolytic** — selectively kills senescent "zombie" cells by inhibiting their survival (SCAP) pathways (**BCL-2 / PI3K-AKT** anti-apoptotic network). Reduced senescent burden and extended median lifespan in aged mice (Mayo Clinic).
**Molecular target:** [BCL2L1 anti-apoptotic (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/598) · [Fisetin (PubChem CID 5281614)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5281614)
**In plain English:** As you age, damaged cells refuse to die and instead sit around leaking inflammation ("zombie cells"). Fisetin selectively kills them. Taken as a monthly 2-day pulse, not daily.
**Protocol:** 500–1,000 mg/day, 2 consecutive days monthly, with fat.
**Bottom line:** Most credible dietary senolytic; low risk, human trials ongoing.

---

### Quercetin + Dasatinib (D+Q)  🟡 (Q) / ⚫🔵 (D, chemo drug) ⭐⭐⭐ (animal) ⭐⭐ (human)
**Goals:** Longevity (senolytic)
**Technical mechanism:** The original senolytic cocktail. **Dasatinib** (a tyrosine-kinase inhibitor, **ABL1/SRC**) kills senescent fat-cell progenitors; **quercetin** (**BCL-2 / PI3K** inhibitor) kills senescent endothelial cells — complementary coverage.
**Molecular target:** [ABL1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/25) · [Dasatinib (PubChem CID 3062316)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/3062316) · [Quercetin (PubChem CID 5280343)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5280343)
**In plain English:** Two zombie-cell killers that cover different cell types together. Dasatinib is a real leukemia chemo drug (prescription, serious) — this combo belongs under medical supervision, not DIY.
**Bottom line:** Foundational senolytic research; dasatinib's toxicity means clinician-only.

---

### Spermidine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (autophagy) ⭐⭐ (human)
**Goals:** Longevity · Heart · Cognition
**Technical mechanism:** Polyamine that induces **autophagy** by inhibiting the acetyltransferase **EP300** and promoting autophagy genes (ATG). Higher dietary intake linked to lower all-cause mortality (20-yr cohort).
**Molecular target:** [EP300 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2033) · [Spermidine (PubChem CID 1102)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/1102)
**In plain English:** Switches on the cell's self-cleaning (autophagy) — clearing junk proteins the way fasting does. Found in wheat germ and aged cheese; people who eat more of it live longer in population studies.
**Protocol:** 1–5 mg/day (or wheat-germ extract).
**Bottom line:** Mechanistically credible, food-derived, growing human data.

---

### Urolithin A  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (muscle mitophagy)
**Goals:** Muscle endurance · Mitochondrial health · Longevity
**Technical mechanism:** Gut-microbiome metabolite of ellagitannins (pomegranate) that induces **mitophagy** — selective recycling of damaged mitochondria — improving muscle mitochondrial quality. Only ~40% of people make it from food, so direct supplementation standardises the dose.
**Molecular target:** [Urolithin A (PubChem CID 5488186)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5488186) · [Examine: Urolithin A](https://examine.com/supplements/urolithin-a/)
**In plain English:** Your muscle cells' power plants get damaged with age; this compound tells the cell to recycle the broken ones. Human trials show improved muscle endurance in older adults. Many people can't make it from pomegranate alone, so the supplement guarantees a dose.
**Protocol:** 500–1,000 mg/day (Mitopure).
**Bottom line:** One of the better-evidenced mitochondrial-longevity supplements.

---

### Ca-AKG (Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate)  🟡 ⭐⭐ (emerging)
**Technical mechanism:** TCA-cycle intermediate and cofactor for **TET enzymes / DNA-demethylases** and prolyl hydroxylases — influences the epigenetic "aging clock." Reduced frailty and biological age markers in a small human study (TruDiagnostic/Ponce de Leon).
**Molecular target:** [Alpha-ketoglutarate (PubChem CID 51)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/51) · [TET2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/54790)
**In plain English:** A cellular-energy molecule that also helps reset the chemical "tags" on your DNA that drift with age. A small human study showed reduced biological age; more needed.
**Protocol:** 1,000 mg/day.
**Bottom line:** Promising epigenetic-aging candidate; early human data.

---

### GlyNAC (Glycine + NAC)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Supplies both substrates for **glutathione** synthesis, restoring the depleted master antioxidant in aging; improved mitochondrial function, insulin sensitivity, and multiple aging hallmarks in Baylor human trials.
**Molecular target:** [GCLC (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2729) · [Glutathione (PubChem CID 124886)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/124886)
**In plain English:** Older bodies run low on glutathione (the main antioxidant). Giving both building blocks restocks it, and small human trials showed improvements across many aging measures at once.
**Protocol:** Glycine ~100 mg/kg + NAC ~100 mg/kg daily (trial doses; high).
**Bottom line:** Among the better human-trial-supported longevity stacks.

---

### Sulforaphane  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Broccoli-sprout isothiocyanate that activates **NRF2 (NFE2L2)** by modifying its inhibitor **KEAP1** — switching on ~200 antioxidant, detox, and anti-inflammatory genes.
**Molecular target:** [NFE2L2/NRF2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4780) · [Sulforaphane (PubChem CID 5350)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5350)
**In plain English:** Turns on your body's master "detox and antioxidant defence" gene program (NRF2). Broccoli sprouts are the richest source; supplements standardise it.
**Protocol:** ~10–40 mg (sprout extract with active myrosinase).
**Bottom line:** Best natural NRF2 activator; good safety.

---

### SGLT2 Inhibitors (Empagliflozin / Canagliflozin)  🟢 (T2DM) / 🟠 (longevity) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (CV) ⭐⭐⭐ (aging)
**Technical mechanism:** Block renal glucose reabsorption transporter **SLC5A2 (SGLT2)**, causing urinary glucose loss, mild caloric deficit, ketone elevation, and reduced senescence/inflammation markers. Canagliflozin extended male-mouse lifespan (ITP).
**Molecular target:** [SLC5A2 SGLT2 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6524) · [Empagliflozin (PubChem CID 11949646)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/11949646)
**In plain English:** Makes you pee out excess sugar, creating a gentle calorie deficit and metabolic-stress benefit. Strong heart and kidney data; emerging longevity signal.
**Watch out:** Genital yeast infections, rare ketoacidosis, dehydration.
**Bottom line:** Strong cardiometabolic evidence; increasingly used off-label for aging.

---

### Astaxanthin  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Carotenoid antioxidant (from algae) that spans cell membranes and quenches singlet oxygen far more potently than vitamin E; activates NRF2, supports mitochondria, skin, and eye health.
**Molecular target:** [Astaxanthin (PubChem CID 5281224)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5281224) · [Examine: astaxanthin](https://examine.com/supplements/astaxanthin/)
**In plain English:** One of nature's strongest antioxidants (what makes salmon pink). Protects cell membranes, skin against UV, and mitochondria.
**Protocol:** 4–12 mg/day with fat.
**Bottom line:** Potent, safe antioxidant with skin and mitochondrial benefits.

---

### Epithalon (Epitalon)  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026; PCAC Jul 2026) ⭐⭐ (Russian data)
**Technical mechanism:** Tetrapeptide reported to activate **telomerase (TERT)**, lengthening telomeres, and to normalise melatonin/circadian rhythm. Most data are older Russian studies.
**Molecular target:** [TERT telomerase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/7015) · [Epitalon (PubChem CID 219042)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/219042)
**In plain English:** Claims to switch on the enzyme that rebuilds the protective caps on your DNA (telomeres). Intriguing but the evidence is largely old, single-group, and hard to verify.
**Watch out:** Telomerase activation is also a cancer concern; weak independent data.
**Bottom line:** Popular in longevity circles; evidence quality is the weak point.

---

### MOTS-c  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026) ⭐⭐ (animal)
**Technical mechanism:** **Mitochondrial-derived peptide** (encoded in mtDNA 12S rRNA) that activates **AMPK**, improves insulin sensitivity and metabolic flexibility, and acts as an exercise mimetic; declines with age.
**Molecular target:** [PRKAA1 AMPK (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5562) · [MOTS-c review (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC13095733/)
**In plain English:** A tiny signal your mitochondria themselves release to say "burn fuel efficiently, adapt like you exercised." It drops with age; supplementing it improved metabolism in animals.
**Bottom line:** Exciting mitochondrial-aging biology; human data pending.

---

### Elamipretide (SS-31)  🔴/🔵(trials) ⭐⭐⭐ (mitochondrial disease)
**Technical mechanism:** Targets and stabilises **cardiolipin** on the inner mitochondrial membrane, restoring electron-transport efficiency and reducing ROS; in trials for mitochondrial myopathy and heart failure.
**Molecular target:** [Elamipretide (PubChem CID 11674452)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/Elamipretide) · [Cardiolipin overview](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/9926)
**In plain English:** Repairs the "wiring insulation" inside your cellular power plants so they make energy cleanly with less damage. Furthest along of the mitochondrial peptides in real trials.
**Bottom line:** Legitimate clinical development for mitochondrial dysfunction.

---

### 17-α-Estradiol  🟠 ⭐⭐ (male-specific animal)
**Technical mechanism:** Weak, non-feminising estrogen stereoisomer; extended **male** mouse lifespan in the ITP without feminisation — likely via hypothalamic inflammation/metabolic effects rather than classic ER signalling.
**Molecular target:** [ESR1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2099) · [17-alpha-estradiol (PubChem CID 68151)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/68151)
**In plain English:** A "mirror-image" estrogen that helped male mice live longer *without* female side effects. Male-specific and still experimental.
**Bottom line:** Curious male-specific longevity signal; human use investigational.

---

### Low-Dose Lithium  🟡/🔵 ⭐⭐ (epidemiology)
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibits **GSK-3β** and induces autophagy/neuroprotection; regions with higher trace lithium in water show lower all-cause and suicide mortality.
**Molecular target:** [GSK3B (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2932) · [Lithium (PubChem CID 3028194)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/3028194)
**In plain English:** At tiny doses (far below psychiatric levels), the mineral lithium may protect the aging brain — populations drinking more of it naturally live a bit longer. Very preliminary.
**Protocol:** Lithium orotate 1–5 mg elemental (microdose).
**Bottom line:** Intriguing neuroprotective microdose signal; not established.

# CATEGORY 10 — COGNITION & NOOTROPICS

From the best-evidenced wakefulness drugs to the experimental "smart drugs."

---

### Modafinil / Armodafinil  ⚫ (Sch IV) / 🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (wakefulness) ⭐⭐ (rested baseline)
**Goals:** Wakefulness · Focus · Fatigue resistance
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibits the **dopamine transporter (SLC6A3/DAT)** (weakly, without amphetamine-like euphoria) and activates **orexin/hypocretin (HCRT)** and histaminergic wake circuits. Armodafinil is the longer-acting R-isomer.
**Molecular target:** [SLC6A3 dopamine transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6531) · [HCRT orexin (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3060) · [Modafinil (PubChem CID 4236)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/4236)
**In plain English:** Switches on the brain's "stay awake" system and gently raises dopamine — sharp, sustained alertness without a jittery high or crash. Dramatic if sleep-deprived; subtler if well-rested.
**Protocol:** 100–200 mg on waking (12–15 h half-life — never afternoon); 2–3×/week max.
**Watch out:** Rare Stevens-Johnson rash (stop on any rash); reduces hormonal contraceptive efficacy (CYP3A4 inducer); headaches.
**Bottom line:** The best-evidenced cognitive enhancer in healthy adults; respect the long half-life.

---

### Racetams (Piracetam, Aniracetam, Oxiracetam, Phenylpiracetam)  🔴/🟡(varies) ⭐⭐⭐ (piracetam, older) ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Positive allosteric modulators of **AMPA glutamate receptors (GRIA)** and enhancers of cholinergic transmission and membrane fluidity. Phenylpiracetam adds a dopaminergic/stimulant edge (and is WADA-banned).
**Molecular target:** [GRIA1 AMPA receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2890) · [Piracetam (PubChem CID 4843)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/4843)
**In plain English:** They make your brain's main "go" signal (glutamate) a bit more responsive and lean on acetylcholine, so memory and clarity sharpen. Pair with a choline source to avoid headaches. Evidence is decent for the elderly, thinner in healthy young people.
**Protocol:** Piracetam 1.6–4.8 g/day + a choline source (alpha-GPC/CDP).
**Bottom line:** Mild, long safety record; take with choline.

---

### Noopept  🔴/🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Dipeptide (racetam-adjacent, ~1000× piracetam potency) that raises **BDNF** and **NGF** expression, is neuroprotective and antioxidant, and lightly stimulates dopamine/ACh.
**Molecular target:** [BDNF brain-derived neurotrophic factor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/627) · [Noopept (PubChem CID 180496)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/180496)
**In plain English:** Boosts your brain's own "growth fertiliser" (BDNF/NGF), supporting learning and protecting neurons, at tiny doses.
**Protocol:** 10–30 mg/day.
**Bottom line:** Potent, cheap, mostly Russian clinical data.

---

### Citicoline (CDP-Choline)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Choline + cytidine → precursor for **acetylcholine** synthesis and for phosphatidylcholine (neuronal membranes); also raises dopamine receptor density. Prescription (Somazina) for stroke in some countries.
**Molecular target:** [CHAT choline acetyltransferase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1103) · [Citicoline (PubChem CID 13804)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/13804)
**In plain English:** Feeds your brain the raw materials to make its "memory and focus" chemical (acetylcholine) and to build cell membranes. Well-tolerated, decent evidence for attention.
**Protocol:** 250–500 mg/day.
**Bottom line:** One of the better-evidenced cholinergic nootropics.

---

### Alpha-GPC  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (cognition, power, GH)
**Technical mechanism:** Most bioavailable choline source crossing the blood-brain barrier → raises **acetylcholine**; also acutely stimulates GH secretion. Used in Europe (Gliatilin) for cognitive decline.
**Molecular target:** [CHAT (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1103) · [Alpha-GPC (PubChem CID 71920)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/71920)
**In plain English:** The most brain-penetrating choline — raw material for the focus/memory chemical, plus a small pre-workout growth-hormone and mind-muscle bump.
**Protocol:** 300–600 mg.
**Watch out:** One observational stroke-risk signal (unconfirmed); fishy odour at high doses.
**Bottom line:** Most potent OTC cholinergic; useful for low-egg diets.
**Stacks with:** Caffeine and huperzine A for a pre-workout / focus stack.
**Avoid:** Too much acetylcholine causes headaches and tension — start low.
**Cost:** ~S$25–40/month.

---

### Lion's Mane (Hericium erinaceus)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Hericenones/erinacines stimulate **Nerve Growth Factor (NGF)** synthesis, supporting neurogenesis and myelination (long-term, not acute).
**Molecular target:** [NGF nerve growth factor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4803) · [Examine: Lion's Mane](https://examine.com/supplements/lions-mane/)
**In plain English:** A mushroom that nudges your brain to produce more "nerve fertiliser" (NGF), supporting new connections over months. Buy fruiting-body extract, not grain filler.
**Protocol:** 500–3,000 mg/day extract.
**Bottom line:** Promising, long-horizon; product quality varies hugely.

---

### Bacopa Monnieri  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (memory, long-term)
**Technical mechanism:** Bacosides enhance acetylcholinesterase modulation and **BDNF**, promote dendritic branching, and are antioxidant — specifically improving memory *consolidation* over 8–12 weeks.
**Molecular target:** [BDNF (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/627) · [Bacopa research — Examine](https://examine.com/supplements/bacopa-monnieri/)
**In plain English:** Improves how well new information sticks — but only after 2–3 months of daily use. Most people quit before it works.
**Protocol:** 300–600 mg (50% bacosides), with fat, 8–12 weeks minimum.
**Bottom line:** Best-evidenced herbal for memory; requires patience.

---

### Semax / Selank  🔴 (reclassified Apr 2026; Semax) ⭐⭐ (Russian data)
**Technical mechanism:** **Semax** (ACTH4-10 analog) raises **BDNF/NGF** and modulates dopamine — focus/neuroprotection. **Selank** (tuftsin analog) modulates **GABA**/serotonin and BDNF — anxiolytic without sedation.
**Molecular target:** [BDNF (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/627) · [Semax (PubChem CID 108987)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/108987)
**In plain English:** Russian nasal-spray peptides — Semax for focus and brain-protection, Selank for calm-focus. Boost the brain's growth factors; mostly Russian clinical use.
**Bottom line:** Interesting, well-tolerated; evidence is largely non-Western.

---

### Methylene Blue  🔵/🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (mitochondrial) ⭐⭐ (cognition)
**Technical mechanism:** At low dose, an alternative **electron carrier** in the mitochondrial electron-transport chain (bypasses damaged complexes to sustain ATP) and a reversible **MAO** inhibitor; antioxidant at low dose, pro-oxidant high. FDA-approved (high dose) for methemoglobinemia.
**Molecular target:** [Methylene blue (PubChem CID 6099)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6099) · [MAOA (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4128)
**In plain English:** A blue dye that acts like a backup wire in your cellular power plants, keeping energy flowing, and gently raises mood chemicals. Low dose only — and it turns your pee green.
**Protocol:** 0.5–2 mg/kg (low dose); pharmaceutical grade only.
**Watch out:** Serotonin syndrome with SSRIs/MAOIs (it's an MAOI); dangerous in G6PD deficiency.
**Bottom line:** Genuine mitochondrial support; respect the drug interactions.

---

### Nicotine (non-combusted)  🟡/⚫(age-restricted) ⭐⭐⭐ (acute focus)
**Technical mechanism:** Agonist at **nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (CHRNA4, CHRNA7)**, enhancing attention, working memory, and reaction time; highly addictive via mesolimbic dopamine.
**Molecular target:** [CHRNA4 nicotinic receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1137) · [Nicotine (PubChem CID 89594)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/89594)
**In plain English:** Genuinely sharpens focus and reaction time by hitting acetylcholine receptors — but it's strongly addictive, so the cognitive upside rarely outweighs the dependence risk. (Divorced from smoking's other harms, but still.)
**Watch out:** Addiction, blood-pressure rise, vasoconstriction.
**Bottom line:** Real acute nootropic, real dependence liability — caution.

---

### L-Tyrosine  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (under acute stress)
**Technical mechanism:** Precursor to **dopamine and norepinephrine** (via tyrosine hydroxylase, **TH**); replenishes catecholamines depleted by acute stress, cold, or sleep loss — restoring performance rather than boosting baseline.
**Molecular target:** [TH tyrosine hydroxylase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/7054) · [L-Tyrosine (PubChem CID 6057)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6057)
**In plain English:** Raw material for your alertness/motivation chemicals. Helps when stress, cold, or no sleep has drained them — does little when you're already fresh.
**Protocol:** 500–2,000 mg before a stressor.
**Bottom line:** Situational — great for stress/sleep-deprivation, not daily baseline.

---

### Bromantane  🔴 ⭐⭐ (Russian data)
**Technical mechanism:** "Actoprotector" that upregulates **tyrosine hydroxylase (TH)** gene expression → increased dopamine synthesis, combined with mild anxiolysis — stimulation without the crash.
**Molecular target:** [TH (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/7054) · [Bromantane (PubChem CID 108030)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/108030)
**In plain English:** Increases how much dopamine you *make* (not just release), giving calm stimulation. Russian anti-fatigue drug; limited Western data.
**Bottom line:** Novel mechanism; sparse independent evidence.

---

### Phenibut  🔴/🟡 ⭐⭐ — dependence risk
**Technical mechanism:** **GABA-B receptor (GABBR1)** agonist (and mild GABA-A / dopamine); anxiolytic and pro-social, but tolerance and physical dependence develop fast.
**Molecular target:** [GABBR1 GABA-B receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2550) · [Phenibut (PubChem CID 14113)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/14113)
**In plain English:** A calming, sociable "GABA" compound — but it hooks you quickly and withdrawal is genuinely nasty. Easy to get into trouble with.
**Watch out:** Rapid tolerance, severe withdrawal. Use rarely if at all.
**Bottom line:** Effective anxiolytic with a real dependence trap — caution.

---

### Dihexa  🔴 ⭐ (animal only)
**Technical mechanism:** Angiotensin-IV-derived peptide that potentiates **hepatocyte growth factor (HGF) / c-Met (MET)** signalling to drive synaptogenesis (new synapses) — extremely potent in rodent memory models.
**Molecular target:** [MET / c-Met receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4233) · [Frontiers peptide review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC13095733/)
**In plain English:** Reportedly builds new brain connections powerfully in animals via a growth-factor pathway. No human data — and growth-factor pathways always carry a tumour question.
**Bottom line:** Striking animal potency, zero human evidence, real theoretical risk.

# CATEGORY 11 — SLEEP

---

### Melatonin  🟡/🔵(EU) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (circadian) ⭐⭐⭐ (insomnia)
**Technical mechanism:** Pineal hormone binding **MT1/MT2 receptors (MTNR1A/MTNR1B)** in the suprachiasmatic nucleus — shifts the circadian clock rather than sedating. Most people massively overdose it.
**Molecular target:** [MTNR1A melatonin receptor 1A (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4543) · [Melatonin (PubChem CID 896)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/896)
**In plain English:** The "it's night" hormone. It re-times your body clock, so it's best for jet lag and late sleep phase, not as a sledgehammer sedative. 0.5 mg works as well as 10 mg with less morning fog.
**Protocol:** 0.3–1 mg, 30–60 min before target sleep.
**Bottom line:** Overused at 10–20× the effective dose. Start at 0.5 mg.
**Stacks with:** Magnesium and glycine for sleep onset.
**Avoid:** Alcohol and other sedatives (additive drowsiness); don’t drive after. A low dose (0.5–1 mg) works better than the common 3–10 mg.
**Cost:** Cheap — ~S$10–20/month (note: pharmacist-only/behind-counter in some SG outlets).

---

### Apigenin  🟡 ⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Chamomile flavonoid, a benzodiazepine-site **GABA-A** ligand (mild) and adenosine modulator; also a mild aromatase inhibitor.
**Molecular target:** [Apigenin (PubChem CID 5280443)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5280443) · [GABRA1 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2554)
**In plain English:** The calming molecule in chamomile tea — a gentle nudge on the brain's "slow down" system for easier sleep onset.
**Protocol:** 50 mg before bed (often with magnesium + glycine).
**Bottom line:** Mild, safe sleep-onset aid.

---

**Magnesium glycinate** (Cat 1), **Glycine** (Cat 1), **L-Theanine** (Cat 1), **Ashwagandha** (Cat 12), **Tart cherry** (Cat 13), **MK-677** (Cat 7, deep sleep). Prescription: **Trazodone** (5-HT2A antagonist), **Gabapentin** (α2δ calcium-channel) — both used off-label for sleep, clinician-directed.

# CATEGORY 12 — STRESS, MOOD & ANXIETY

---

### Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Withanolides act on the **HPA axis** to lower cortisol and modulate **GABA** receptors; reduces the cortisol-driven suppression of testosterone.
**Molecular target:** [NR3C1 glucocorticoid receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2908) · [Withanolide A (PubChem CID 11294368)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/11294368)
**In plain English:** Turns down the stress-hormone (cortisol) thermostat, which improves sleep, calm, and — because stress suppresses testosterone — modestly raises T in stressed men.
**Protocol:** 300–600 mg/day KSM-66 or Sensoril.
**Watch out:** Stimulates thyroid; caution in autoimmune disease/thyroid meds; rare liver reports.
**Bottom line:** Best-evidenced adaptogen for stress, sleep, and hormones.
**Avoid:** Sedatives and benzodiazepines (additive drowsiness), thyroid medication (it can raise thyroid hormone), and immunosuppressants (it’s immune-stimulating). Skip in pregnancy.
**Cost:** ~S$20–40/month for a standardised extract (KSM-66 or Sensoril). Guardian, Watsons, iHerb.

---

### Rhodiola Rosea  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Rosavins/salidroside modulate the HPA axis and raise norepinephrine/serotonin/dopamine — *energising* anti-fatigue (unlike calming ashwagandha).
**Molecular target:** [Salidroside (PubChem CID 159278)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/159278) · [Examine: Rhodiola](https://examine.com/supplements/rhodiola-rosea/)
**In plain English:** The "energising" adaptogen — cuts mental and physical fatigue under stress. Morning use; too stimulating at night.
**Protocol:** 200–400 mg (3% rosavins) morning.
**Bottom line:** Best adaptogen for fatigue and heavy training blocks.
**Avoid:** Take it early — it can be stimulating and disrupt sleep late in the day; caution with other stimulants and with antidepressants (MAOIs).
**Cost:** ~S$20–35/month; standardised to ~3% rosavins / 1% salidroside.

---

### Saffron (Crocus sativus)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (mild-moderate depression)
**Technical mechanism:** Crocin/safranal inhibit serotonin reuptake and are anti-inflammatory/antioxidant; several RCTs rival low-dose SSRIs for mild-moderate depression.
**Molecular target:** [Crocin (PubChem CID 5281233)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/5281233) · [SLC6A4 serotonin transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6532)
**In plain English:** The spice mildly raises serotonin like a gentle antidepressant, with surprisingly good trial support for low mood.
**Protocol:** 30 mg/day standardised (affron/Satiereal).
**Bottom line:** Genuinely evidence-backed mood support.

---

### 5-HTP / L-Tryptophan  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Direct precursors to **serotonin** (5-HTP bypasses the rate-limiting step); tryptophan also → melatonin.
**Molecular target:** [TPH2 tryptophan hydroxylase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/121278) · [5-HTP (PubChem CID 144)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/144)
**In plain English:** Raw material for serotonin (mood/sleep). Useful, but don't combine with antidepressants (serotonin syndrome), and take with B6.
**Watch out:** Never with SSRIs/MAOIs. Cycle to avoid depleting dopamine.
**Bottom line:** Effective for mood/sleep alone; dangerous with serotonergic drugs.

---

### Also for stress: **L-Theanine**, **Magnesium** (Cat 1), **Selank** (Cat 10), **Inositol** (panic/PCOS, 12–18 g/day), **Kava** (potent GABAergic anxiolytic — real hepatotoxicity risk, caution).

# CATEGORY 13 — JOINT & CONNECTIVE TISSUE

---

### Collagen Peptides (+ Vitamin C)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Hydrolysed collagen supplies glycine/proline/hydroxyproline that preferentially accumulate in tendon/ligament; taken with vitamin C (cofactor for **prolyl hydroxylase**) before loading, it doubles collagen-synthesis markers.
**Molecular target:** [COL1A1 collagen type I (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1277) · [P4HA1 prolyl hydroxylase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/5033)
**In plain English:** Feeds your tendons and ligaments their raw materials right before you stress them — timing matters, so take it ~45 min *before* training with vitamin C.
**Protocol:** 15 g + 50 mg vitamin C, 45–60 min pre-exercise.
**Bottom line:** The most practical connective-tissue supplement; timing is key. Highly relevant to running/Hyrox tendon load.
**Stacks with:** Vitamin C — required to build collagen, so take them together, ideally 45–60 min before training tendons.
**Cost:** ~S$25–45/month; unflavoured hydrolysed peptides mix into anything.

---

### Glucosamine + Chondroitin  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (moderate OA)
**Technical mechanism:** Substrates for cartilage glycosaminoglycans; chondroitin inhibits cartilage-degrading enzymes (aggrecanases/MMPs). Glucosamine sulphate also linked to lower all-cause mortality in cohorts.
**Molecular target:** [Glucosamine (PubChem CID 439213)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/439213) · [Examine](https://examine.com/supplements/glucosamine/)
**In plain English:** Building blocks and protectors for cartilage. Reduces pain in moderate arthritis over ~8–12 weeks; it's structural, not a painkiller.
**Protocol:** Glucosamine sulphate 1,500 mg + chondroitin 1,200 mg/day.
**Bottom line:** Meaningful for moderate OA; give it 3 months.
**Avoid:** Shellfish allergy (most glucosamine is shellfish-derived); mild interaction with warfarin.
**Cost:** ~S$20–40/month; give it 6–8 weeks before judging.

---

### Boswellia Serrata  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Boswellic acids (AKBA) inhibit **5-lipoxygenase (ALOX5)**, blocking pro-inflammatory leukotrienes — a different anti-inflammatory pathway from NSAIDs.
**Molecular target:** [ALOX5 5-lipoxygenase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/240) · [AKBA (PubChem CID 11168387)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/11168387)
**In plain English:** Frankincense extract that blocks a specific inflammation pathway NSAIDs miss — good for joint pain, often paired with curcumin.
**Protocol:** 100–250 mg AKBA-standardised.
**Bottom line:** Solid anti-inflammatory for joints; stacks with curcumin.

---

# CATEGORY 14 — CARDIOVASCULAR

### Citrus Bergamot  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐

**Bottom line:** Reasonable natural lipid support.

### Beetroot / Dietary Nitrate  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Nitrate → (oral bacteria) → nitrite → **nitric oxide** via NOS-independent pathway; vasodilation, lower O₂ cost of exercise, lower BP.

**In plain English:** Beet juice widens blood vessels so exercise costs less oxygen and blood pressure drops — but *don't use antibacterial mouthwash*, which kills the bacteria that make it work.

**Bottom line:** Well-evidenced endurance and BP aid; mind the mouthwash.

## Additions to CATEGORY 1 — Foundational (essential vitamins/minerals)

### Vitamin C (Ascorbate)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Cofactor for prolyl/lysyl hydroxylases (**P4HA1**) in collagen synthesis, for dopamine-β-hydroxylase (noradrenaline), and a water-phase antioxidant regenerating vitamin E.
**Molecular target:** [SLC23A1 vitamin C transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/9963) · [Ascorbic acid (PubChem CID 54670067)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/54670067)
**In plain English:** Your body literally can't build collagen without it, and it mops up free radicals. Take it with collagen for tendons.
**Protocol:** 200–1,000 mg/day. **Bottom line:** Foundational; deficiency impairs connective tissue and immunity.
**Stacks with:** Iron (boosts absorption of plant iron) and collagen (it's the cofactor that builds collagen).
**Cost:** Cheap — ~S$10–20/month; split the dose, large single doses aren't better absorbed.

### B-Complex / B12 / Methylfolate / B6  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (deficiency)
**Technical mechanism:** Coenzymes in one-carbon metabolism and methylation (**MTHFR** converts folate to active 5-MTHF), homocysteine clearance, energy metabolism, and neurotransmitter synthesis. B12 (methylcobalamin) is depleted by metformin and in vegans.
**Molecular target:** [MTHFR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/4524) · [Methylcobalamin (PubChem CID 6436231)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/6436231)
**In plain English:** The spark plugs of energy metabolism and the "methylation" chemistry that runs DNA, mood, and detox. Use methylfolate/methyl-B12 (active forms) especially if you have an MTHFR gene variant.
**Bottom line:** Correct deficiency (vegans, metformin users); methylated forms preferred.

### Iron  🟡/🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (deficiency)
**Technical mechanism:** Core of **hemoglobin** (O₂ transport) and myoglobin; cofactor for mitochondrial enzymes. Deficiency (common in menstruating women and endurance athletes) causes fatigue and poor endurance long before anemia shows.
**Molecular target:** [TF transferrin / HBB hemoglobin (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3043) · [Iron (PubChem CID 23925)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/23925)
**In plain English:** Iron carries oxygen in your blood — low iron means gasping endurance and constant tiredness. Test ferritin before supplementing; too much iron is toxic.
**Watch out:** Never supplement without testing — iron overload damages organs. **Bottom line:** Test-then-treat; critical for female and endurance athletes.

### Iodine / Selenium  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (thyroid)
**Technical mechanism:** Iodine is the substrate for thyroid hormones T3/T4; selenium (via **GPX** and deiodinase **DIO1/2**) converts T4→active T3 and protects the thyroid.
**Molecular target:** [DIO2 deiodinase (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/1734) · [Iodine (PubChem CID 807)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/807)
**In plain English:** Your thyroid (the body's metabolic thermostat) is built from iodine and switched on by selenium. Deficiency slows your whole metabolism.
**Bottom line:** Foundational thyroid pair; don't megadose iodine.

### Niacin / NADH (Vitamin B3)  🟡 ⭐⭐⭐ (NAD precursor, lipids)
**Technical mechanism:** Direct **NAD⁺** precursor (salvage pathway); high-dose niacin also raises HDL and lowers Lp(a) via hepatic effects.
**Molecular target:** [NAMPT (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/10135) · [Niacin (PubChem CID 938)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/938)
**In plain English:** The cheapest way to raise NAD⁺ (your cellular energy coenzyme) — though it causes a hot "flush." NMN/NR are the flush-free modern versions.
**Bottom line:** Cheap NAD⁺ route; flushing limits it.

**Bottom line:** Always pair with D3 + K2; don't megadose alone.

### Strontium · Silica (brief)

## Additions to CATEGORY 10 — Cognition (prescription stimulants + more)

### Amphetamine (Adderall) / Lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse)  ⚫ (Sch II) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (ADHD)
**Technical mechanism:** Release dopamine/norepinephrine and block their reuptake via **DAT (SLC6A3)** and **NET (SLC6A2)**, and reverse-transport through VMAT2 — potent focus/wakefulness, high addiction/cardiac risk. Vyvanse is a prodrug (abuse-resistant).
**Molecular target:** [SLC6A3 dopamine transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6531) · [Amphetamine (PubChem CID 3007)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/3007)
**In plain English:** Floods the brain's focus/motivation chemicals — extremely effective for ADHD, extremely habit-forming and hard on the heart if misused. Schedule II.
**Watch out:** Dependence, cardiovascular strain, tolerance. **Bottom line:** Powerful, legitimate for ADHD, high misuse liability.

### Methylphenidate (Ritalin)  ⚫ (Sch II) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (ADHD)
**Technical mechanism:** Blocks DAT/NET reuptake (without amphetamine's release mechanism) — milder pharmacology, same therapeutic use.
**Molecular target:** [SLC6A3 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6531) · [Methylphenidate (PubChem CID 4158)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/4158)
**In plain English:** Raises dopamine by blocking its reabsorption — the other main ADHD drug, a bit gentler than Adderall.
**Bottom line:** First-line ADHD stimulant; controlled.

### Huperzine A · Ginkgo · Panax Ginseng · Uridine (brief)

### Orexin Antagonists (Suvorexant, Lemborexant)  🔵 ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Additions to CATEGORY 12 — Mood (the actual psychiatry)

### SSRIs (Sertraline, Escitalopram)  🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Block the **serotonin transporter (SLC6A4)**, raising synaptic serotonin; downstream BDNF/neuroplasticity changes over weeks explain the delayed benefit.
**Molecular target:** [SLC6A4 serotonin transporter (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/6532) · [Sertraline (PubChem CID 68617)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/68617)
**In plain English:** Keep serotonin in the synapse longer; over weeks this rewires mood circuits. First-line for depression/anxiety — real, evidence-based medicine.
**Bottom line:** Standard of care; the herbal options don't replace these for moderate-severe illness.

### Ketamine / Esketamine (Spravato)  🔵/⚫ ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (resistant depression)
**Technical mechanism:** **NMDA-receptor (GRIN)** antagonist triggering a glutamate surge → rapid synaptogenesis via BDNF/mTOR — antidepressant within hours, not weeks. Esketamine is FDA-approved for treatment-resistant depression.
**Molecular target:** [GRIN1 NMDA receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/2902) · [Ketamine (PubChem CID 3821)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/3821)
**In plain English:** Rapidly regrows brain connections lost in depression — works in hours where SSRIs take weeks. Dissociative and controlled, used under supervision.
**Bottom line:** Breakthrough for resistant depression; clinic-administered.

### Psilocybin / Microdosing  🔵(trials)/⚫ ⭐⭐⭐ (macro, trials) ⭐⭐ (microdosing)
**Technical mechanism:** **5-HT2A serotonin receptor (HTR2A)** agonist driving neuroplasticity (BDNF, dendritic growth); breakthrough-therapy status for depression. Microdosing evidence is far weaker than full-dose therapy.
**Molecular target:** [HTR2A 5-HT2A receptor (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3356) · [Psilocybin (PubChem CID 10624)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/10624)
**In plain English:** Full-dose (with therapy) shows real, lasting antidepressant effects via a burst of brain plasticity. Sub-perceptual "microdosing" is mostly placebo in controlled trials.
**Bottom line:** Macro-dose therapy is the real signal; microdosing hype outruns data.

## Additions to CATEGORY 14 — Cardiovascular (the actual drugs)

### Statins (Atorvastatin/Rosuvastatin)  🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Inhibit **HMG-CoA reductase (HMGCR)**, the rate-limiting enzyme of cholesterol synthesis → upregulated LDL receptors clear LDL from blood; also anti-inflammatory (lower CRP).
**Molecular target:** [HMGCR (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/3156) · [Atorvastatin (PubChem CID 60823)](https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/60823)
**In plain English:** Turn down your liver's cholesterol factory so it pulls LDL out of your blood instead. The most evidence-backed drugs for preventing heart attacks. Pair with CoQ10 (statins deplete it).
**Bottom line:** Cornerstone of cardiovascular prevention.

### PCSK9 Inhibitors (Evolocumab, Inclisiran)  🟢 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Technical mechanism:** Block **PCSK9**, the protein that degrades LDL receptors — so LDL receptors survive longer and clear far more LDL. Inclisiran is an siRNA silencing PCSK9, dosed twice yearly.
**Molecular target:** [PCSK9 (NCBI Gene)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/255738) · [Evolocumab (DrugBank)](https://go.drugbank.com/drugs/DB09303)
**In plain English:** LDL receptors are the vacuum cleaners that remove bad cholesterol; PCSK9 destroys them. Block PCSK9 and the vacuums keep working — dropping LDL dramatically. Inclisiran is a gene-silencing shot given just twice a year.
**Bottom line:** Most powerful LDL-lowering available; the siRNA form is a genuine leap.

### Red Yeast Rice · Ezetimibe (brief)
**Red yeast rice** (contains natural monacolin K = lovastatin — a "supplement statin," ⭐⭐⭐⭐, same risks); **Ezetimibe** (blocks intestinal cholesterol absorption via **NPC1L1**, [gene](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gene/29881) — ⭐⭐⭐⭐, pairs with statins).
